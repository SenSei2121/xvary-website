ServiceNow's Beat-and-Raise Crushed by AI Panic Selloff

The market saw disruption fears and sold 16-18%. The numbers told a different story: accelerating demand and AI as the accelerator, not the killer.

xvary research desk · april 24, 2026 · ~3 min read

You watched software names crater Thursday after ServiceNow and IBM reported. ServiceNow shares plunged over 16%, dragging Salesforce, Workday, and Oracle lower on fresh AI-disruption panic. Consensus narrative: legacy workflow platforms face replacement by pure AI-native tools, customers delay big spends amid macro and geopolitical noise, and growth caps out. The selloff looked like confirmation. Reality delivered the punchline: ServiceNow beat and raised while the market punished results that proved AI is supercharging its platform, not burying it.

Q1 told the real tale. Total revenue hit $3.77 billion, up 22% year-over-year and beating estimates by roughly $20-30 million. Subscription revenue landed at $3.671 billion—up 22% reported, 19% in constant currency—exceeding the high end of guidance despite a 75-basis-point headwind from delayed Middle East on-premise deals tied to regional conflict. That beat didn't happen in a vacuum. Customers are leaning harder into ServiceNow's platform precisely because it layers AI agents directly into existing enterprise workflows faster than standalone AI can rip them out and rebuild from scratch.

The guidance raise sealed it. ServiceNow lifted full-year 2026 subscription revenue outlook by $205 million at the midpoint to $15.735-$15.775 billion, pointing to 20.5-21% constant-currency growth. That includes about 125 basis points from the Armis acquisition, yet management still signaled confidence in organic momentum. Current remaining performance obligations (cRPO) grew 21% in constant currency to $12.64 billion, beating internal targets by 100 basis points. These aren't soft signals—they're contracted revenue that will hit the top line over the next twelve months, and they're accelerating while the street fixates on headlines.

Now Assist, ServiceNow's flagship AI offering, drove the clearest proof point. Management hiked the 2026 revenue target from $1 billion to $1.5 billion, citing 'stunning' enterprise adoption and over 130% year-over-year growth in customers spending more than $1 million on Now Assist. Deals bundling three or more Now Assist products jumped nearly 70%. Sixteen transactions topped $5 million in net new annual contract value, including five above $10 million—an 80% increase in large-deal count. New-logo ACV growth accelerated above 50%. Enterprises aren't pausing for disruption; they're paying up for AI that delivers measurable workflow outcomes inside the platform they already trust.

Margin noise and Armis dilution provided convenient cover for the panic, but dig in and the capital allocation story sharpens the contrast. Subscription gross margins held firm near 81-82% levels pre-integration, with guidance at 81.5% for the year despite a modest 25-basis-point Armis headwind. Operating margin came in at 32%, 50 basis points above plan. Free cash flow margin reached 44%. ServiceNow executed a $2 billion accelerated share repurchase plus open-market buys, retiring roughly 20.1 million shares in the quarter—more than double the total repurchased in all of 2025. They're buying back stock aggressively while delivering beats, managing dilution, and positioning for AI pull-through that management believes will compound into 2027 and beyond.

The deadpan fact bomb that reframes everything: ServiceNow beat estimates, raised full-year subscription guidance, and hiked its AI target while IBM merely held steady—yet both stocks and the broader software cohort dropped double digits. The market didn't price execution; it priced one lazy narrative that AI will cannibalize workflow platforms. ServiceNow's data says the opposite: its control-tower architecture makes AI actionable at enterprise scale today, turning potential disruptors into demand drivers. The 16-18% plunge wasn't valuation discipline. It was overreaction to temporary noise—75 basis points of geopolitics and acquisition accounting—while ignoring the demand acceleration right in front of investors.

You don't need perfect macro conditions for this setup to work. You need continued proof that large enterprises keep signing bigger, faster AI-inclusive deals on the ServiceNow platform. The selloff handed anyone paying attention an entry point into a company executing exactly where the consensus claims weakness. Ignore the noise, watch the numbers, and the direction becomes obvious.

hero stat
Q1 subscription revenue: $3.671B (+19% CC, beat high-end guidance) | cRPO: $12.64B (+21% CC, +100bps beat) | FY2026 sub rev guide raised $205M midpoint to $15.735-15.775B (20.5-21% CC) | Now Assist 2026 target: $1.5B (up from $1B)
kill criteria
  • Q2 2026 subscription revenue growth falls below 19% CC or cRPO growth misses the 19.5% CC guidance.
  • FY2026 AI revenue run-rate tracks below $1.2B by the Q3 report in August 2026.
  • Subscription gross margin breaks below 80% for two consecutive quarters post-Armis integration.
  • Management cuts full-year guidance or cites sustained customer pauses in large deals by Q3.
verdict

Buy the dip. ServiceNow is the AI workflow layer enterprises are deploying today, not the disruption victim the market priced in. The 16-18% selloff was a gift.
