michael burry is buying nvidia puts. our kill criteria says he might be right.

we track 6 kill criteria on nvidia. one just triggered. four are in the watch zone. here is the scorecard.

xvary research desk · march 25, 2026 · ~3 min read

Michael burry is back. The man who shorted the housing market before 2008 is now buying put options on nvidia and palantir. His thesis: the AI trade is a bubble, credit stress is building ($1.2 trillion in credit card debt), and the market is pricing perfection in a world that does not deliver it.

That is not a new take. What is new is that our data agrees with part of it.

the scorecard. we track 6 kill criteria on nvidia. these are the specific, measurable conditions that would break the bull thesis. not "regulatory risk" or "competition" — named thresholds with current values. here is where they stand today:

1. free cash flow yield: DANGER. threshold: 3.0%. current: 2.4%. nvidia is generating less free cash per dollar of stock price than our minimum. at current prices, the stock is not paying you enough in cash generation to justify the valuation. this is the one burry is right about.

2. gross margin: WATCH. threshold: 65.0%. current: 71.1%. comfortable — but narrowing. nine percentage points of cushion sounds like a lot until you realize it was fifteen points eighteen months ago. the direction matters as much as the distance.

3. operating margin: WATCH. threshold: 55.0%. current: 60.4%. same story. safe today. the trend is what you watch.

4. revenue growth: WATCH. threshold: 43.6% YoY. current: 65.5% YoY. still well above. but the market is pricing acceleration, not deceleration. if growth slows to 44% — still extraordinary by any normal standard — the thesis is under pressure because the multiple assumes much more.

5. ROIC: SAFE. threshold: 50.0%. current: 70.3%. the moat is intact. nvidia still generates exceptional returns on invested capital. this is the strongest pillar of the bull case.

6. EV/Revenue: WATCH. threshold: 15.0x. current: 19.8x. trading at nearly 20 times revenue. that is not cheap. but it was 30x a year ago, so the compression has already started.

the honest assessment. burry is right about one thing: nvidia's valuation does not leave room for error. a free cash flow yield below 3% means the stock price has outrun the company's ability to generate cash. that is a real concern, not a vibes call.

But the full picture is more nuanced than "nvidia is a bubble." the company still has 70% ROIC, 71% gross margins, and 65% revenue growth. Four of six kill criteria are in the watch zone — approaching their thresholds but not there yet. The thesis is stressed, not dead.

what would make burry wrong. if nvidia's next two quarters show revenue growth above 43% and gross margin holds above 65%, the thesis survives. the market will forgive a low FCF yield if the growth engine keeps delivering. growth covers a lot of sins in valuation.

what would make him right. if gross margin drops below 65% for two quarters — meaning competition or pricing pressure is real — and revenue growth decelerates below the market-implied 43% pace, the multiple compresses hard. at that point, burry's puts print.

The next earnings report is the decision point. Nvidia reports in late may. That is 60 days away. Both sides of this trade are betting on the same filing. The difference is which line they are watching.

kill criteria
  • Nvidia gross margin drops below 65% for two consecutive quarters — competition or pricing pressure is real.
  • Revenue growth decelerates below 43% YoY — the market-implied pace. The multiple compresses.
  • Next two quarters show revenue growth above 43% AND gross margin above 65% — burry's thesis is wrong and the puts expire worthless.
  • ROIC drops below 50% — the moat is eroding, not just the valuation.
verdict

Burry is right about FCF yield. He is early on everything else. Four of six kill criteria are watching, not breached. Nvidia reports in late may — that filing decides whether this is a stressed thesis or a broken one. If you own nvidia, you do not need to sell. You need to know exactly which numbers matter in the next earnings report: gross margin above 65% and revenue growth above 43%. Those are the lines.
