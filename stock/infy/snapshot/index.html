What just happened
missed estimates
Last earnings came in at $0.18 per share versus a $0.22 estimate, a clean reminder that stable businesses can still disappoint.
Consensus shows an ~18% EPS miss on ADR figures ($0.18 vs ~$0.22) even as Q3 revenue was about $5.1B and beat expectations—top line and bottom line can diverge when costs and headcount run hot. The ~30% profitability line in this snapshot is operating margin, not gross margin; do not trust a feed that labels ~30.5% as “gross” next to a 30% operating margin without checking the filing.

the number that mattered
The key number was the 18.18% EPS miss, because a stock trading at 24.2x earnings does not get to miss quietly.
  • infosys limited’s stock price is up roughly 10% in value following solid december-period results (fiscal year ends march 31, 2026).
    the global it, cloud, and ai solutions provider posted revenues of $5.1 billion for the third quarter, exceeding consensus expectations. the performance was underpinned by rising enterprise demand for ai-related solutions, as well as strong large deal activity in previous periods.
  • moreover, the investment community seemed to applaud management’s upbeat near-term operating outlook.
    note, however, that the adrs remain ranked to mirror the broader market averages in the year ahead (timeliness: 3).
  • our profit forecasts for fiscal 2025 and 2026 are being tempered slightly.
    infosys reported earnings of $0.18 per adr for the december period, with the figure slipping a penny vs. prior year, and falling short of our $0.22 mark. higher cost of sales and sg&a expenses, largely due to increased headcount, likely weighed on the result. while reduced operating costs and stock buybacks ought to support improved profits in the near term, we are scaling back our current fiscal year estimate by a nickel, to $0.80 per adr.
  • likewise, our fiscal 2026 bottom-line call is being lowered $0.10, to $0.95 per adr.
    management’s push into the ai arena, coupled with accelerating customer demand trends, augurs well for long-term business prospects. indeed, infosys is well positioned to support overall enterprise adoption of ai-related solutions via its topaz platform.
  • global business spending on digital transformations, which includes the implementation of cloud and ai to boost operating efficiencies, network security, and customer and employee experiences, is expected to surge in the years ahead.
