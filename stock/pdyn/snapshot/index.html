What they do
It sells AI software that helps robots and unmanned machines work in messy real-world settings without constant human control.
Palladyne is trying to be the software layer inside other companies' robots, not the robot maker itself. That matters because its tools are built for minimal integration effort, which means your customer does not need to rebuild the whole machine to use them. With just 71 employees and only $9 million of long-term debt, it is small enough to move fast and light enough to survive mistakes.

If that jump does not start showing up in quarterly results, the market is left valuing promises instead of sales.

Losses that dwarf revenue
PDYN reported a $49.7M net loss on $5.2M of revenue in 2025. That's a n/a net margin.
Even with only $9M of long-term debt, losses this large can force capital raises or delay the path to self-funded growth.

Thin disclosure on product economics
Management has not broken out revenue by Palladyne IQ versus SwarmOS, and the snapshot does not show gross margin targets for 2026.
That makes it hard to judge whether future revenue is high-quality software revenue or just one-off project activity.
At ~$322M market cap, this stock is priced for a business far larger than the current $5.2M revenue base. If the 2026 ramp slips, the narrative does too.
