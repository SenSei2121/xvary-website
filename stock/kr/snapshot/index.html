the number that mattered
The 2.6% identical-sales gain mattered because a grocery chain with a 2.2% net margin cannot afford weak traffic.
  • kroger has been producing solid results.
  • october-quarter earnings were consistent with our estimate, as profits rose 7% vs. prior year, to $1.05 a share. (this excludes $2.0 billion in impairment and other charges related to e-commerce operations, more below.) revenues increased less than 1%, partly reflecting last year’s sale of the specialty pharmacy business.
  • however, identical sales (excluding fuel) were up a respectable 2.6%, and the gross margin expanded by nearly 50 basis points.
    the company made only minor changes to its fiscal-2025 outlook, which implies that vs. prior year gains for both identical sales and earnings in the january quarter will be similar to the october period. (fiscal 2025 ends january 31st.) the company is adjusting its ecommerce strategy.
  • sales from online purchases continue to rise at a strong clip, including growth of 17% in the october quarter.
    these operations, though, remain a drag on the bottom line, and management has announced various initiatives aimed at shortening the pathway to profitability. in particular, kroger is reducing its footprint of automated fulfillment centers, while leaning more heavily on its existing stores and third-party delivery providers, including instacart, its primary partner, to complete online transactions. these moves should generate about $400 million in profitability improvements and help bring these operations into the black in the upcoming fiscal year. still, competition for market share is likely to remain intense in this space, particularly from amazon, which has been expanding foodshopping options for its prime customers.
  • we look for earnings to advance 8%–10% in fiscal 2026.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is e-commerce profitability after a $2.0B reset.

!
high
e-commerce profitability reset
Kroger took $2.0B in impairment and related charges tied to e-commerce operations. That tells you the old digital playbook was too expensive.
If the promised $400M in profitability improvements does not show up, the market will question the entire digital strategy.
∘
med
thin-margin grocery economics
A 2.1% net margin means Kroger does not have much cushion against price competition, labor pressure, or higher fulfillment costs.
Small operating misses can matter fast when you only keep about 2 cents of every revenue dollar.
∘
med
albertsons litigation over the failed merger
Kroger has filed counterclaims in its dispute with Albertsons. This is no longer about strategic upside. It is about legal drag and management attention.
Even without a deal, a long court fight can create cost, distraction, and headline risk.
~
low
balance sheet flexibility
The balance sheet is rated B+, with $16.1B in long-term debt and 29% of capital tied to debt. That's manageable, not carefree.
If operating pressure rises at the same time digital spending stays elevated, flexibility narrows.
A grocery retailer with a 2.1% net margin cannot afford repeated digital misfires. The e-commerce charge already showed what happens when execution slips.
