The 100% EPS beat matters because the market expected $0.08 and got $0.16, which says cost control is doing real work.
  • sustained momentum in macau and cost discipline paid off last year.
  • demand rebounded sharply, with october delivering record mass table performance at city of dreams and continued gross gaming revenue growth versus the prior year.
    incremental gaming capacity, premium mass initiatives, and improved customer engagement supported volumes without sacrificing margins.
  • cost controls remained effective, despite seasonal promotions and one-time events.
    outside macau, cyprus likely delivered another strong quarter, while balance-sheet actions reduced refinancing risk, supporting earnings quality and operational stability.
  • this year should be solid, though growth may be tempered a bit.
    we expect the hotelier to benefit from continued strength in premium mass gaming in macau, incremental capacity from reallocated tables and machines, and ongoing product enhancements at city of dreams and studio city. the reopening of the renovated countdown hotel should boost visitation, non-gaming spend, and mix improvement.
  • internationally, cyprus is expected to scale further, while sri lanka gradually ramps.
