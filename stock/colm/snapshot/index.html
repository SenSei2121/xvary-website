What just happened
beat estimates
Columbia beat by 21%: $1.73 in EPS versus $1.43 expected.
Revenue was $2.3B, up 147% vs. prior year, and gross margin, the profit left after product costs, was 50.1%. The beat came from profit control as much as sales.

the number that mattered
50.1% gross margin kept half the sales dollar after product costs. That is why EPS beat by 21%.
  • columbia sportswear reported mixed results in the september interim.
  • sales of $943 million grew by about 1% from the prior-year level, and maintained the pace of vs. prior year advances for the fourth straight quarter.
  • however, the operating margin fell by two percentage points, to 11.5%, even after adjusting for significant asset impairments (discussed below).
  • adjusted earnings of $1.41 per share fell about 10% from last year's $1.56, but was above our $1.19 call.
  • recent charges for product lines reflect continued weak demand.
    in the third quarter, columbia marked down the asset values of prana and mountain hardwear units following multiple years of languishing results. of the total $29 million in non-cash charges, intangible assets associated with prana were reduced by $17 million (including $8 million for the trade name) and Mountain Hardwear's assets were written down by around $12 million. although the expenses have little direct impact on cash flow, the reduction in future amortization charges related to the prana trade name ought to provide a modest benefit to bottom-line results.
