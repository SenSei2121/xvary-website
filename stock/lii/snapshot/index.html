the number that mattered
The $4.06 print was 43.6% below the $7.2 estimate.
  • management tempered its 2025 outlook for lennox international in late october.
  • leadership conceded that overall sales at the texas-based hvac maker were likely to decline 1% for the year, versus its previous expectation of a 3% rise in the top line and the outsized 13% growth that the company enjoyed in 2024.
  • at the same time, the executive team lowered its target range for adjusted earnings per share to $22.75–$23.25, implying cost-driven bottom-line growth of 1%–3%, compared to its previous $23.25–$24.25 goal and the 3%–7% advance that it represented.
    comparisons are a bit easier this year, as lennox gets a full 12-month’s contribution from the parts and accessories business that it acquired from nsi in october.
  • that deal alone adds about $225 million in annual revenue to the company’s top line.
    the hvac maker also stands to benefit from a joint venture with samsung that is set to kick in, as well as from the work down of what recently were bloated inventories within the distributor channel. that said, we do worry about the financial health of households in general and the uncertain prospects for residential construction, given mortgage-rate fluctuations. lennox doesn’t make advanced cooling solutions for the growing number of data centers set to handle massive artificial intelligence workloads. what’s more, management hinted that it’s not likely to pursue the opportunity anytime soon, if at all. subscribers looking for hvac makers with data center exposure should consider aaon, inc. and trane technologies, among others.
  • the company ought to generate a growing amount of excess cash.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is softer HVAC demand after management cut its own outlook.

∘
med
demand slowdown sticks
management already said overall sales were likely to decline and lowered adjusted EPS guidance to $22. if that caution is the new baseline, the recovery story gets delayed.
the stock is priced on quality. quality with slower demand still works, but the multiple usually compresses.
∘
med
distributor inventory takes longer to clear
management highlighted the work-down of previously bloated channel inventories. if distributors stay cautious, orders lag even if end demand stabilizes.
that turns a short reset into several slower quarters, which matters when analysts still model $6.0B of revenue.
∘
med
NSI and samsung execution disappoints
the public growth offsets on this page are the ~$225M NSI revenue addition and the samsung joint venture. if those underdeliver, the market loses two of the cleaner reasons to look past the slowdown.
you are left with the legacy HVAC cycle doing all the work — and that is a less exciting setup.
This is still a concentrated story: 100% of the $5.2B revenue base shown on this page ties back to HVAC and refrigeration demand.
