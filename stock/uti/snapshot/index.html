the number that mattered
The guided EPS step-down while revenue climbs is the investment story: growth capex hits GAAP earnings before the new campuses mature.
  • universal technical institute is making its debut in the institutional data.
  • founded in 1965, the company provides training programs and workforce education.
    it operates through two divisions: universal technical institute (uti) and concorde career colleges (concorde). uti offers degree and non-degree programs in transportation, skilled trades, and energy technical training across 15 campuses in nine states. concorde provides healthcare education programs, including allied health, dental, nursing, and diagnostic fields, through online platforms and 17 campuses in eight states.
  • both divisions emphasize hands-on learning, classroom delivery, and blended models.
    the company looks well positioned to address a growing demand for skilled trades and healthcare careers. with plans to open three new campuses in fiscal 2026 (year ends september 30th), the arizona-based company is expanding into active regions including fort myers, florida; atlanta, georgia; and san antonio, texas. additionally, universal technical institute is set to launch approximately 20 new programs, targeting areas with significant workforce shortages such as nursing, dental hygiene, renewable energy, and advanced manufacturing. these strategic initiatives should align with employer demand, and ought to help position universal technical institute as a key player in addressing the national skills gap.
  • we have a favorable outlook on universal technical institute.
  • for fiscal 2026, we expect revenue of approximately $910 million, which represents vs. prior year growth of 9%.
    while the company is increasing investments for campus expansions and program development, these upfront costs will likely moderate earnings this year.
