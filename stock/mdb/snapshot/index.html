the number that mattered
The $1.65 versus -$0.09 earnings gap mattered most because it showed MongoDB can blow past expectations when cloud demand cooperates.
  • it was a banner year for mongodb's stockholders in 2025.
  • the share price rose more than 75%, as the firm's atlas cloud-based offering and vector search technology seems to be becoming the standard database used for artificial intelligence (ai) applications.
    developers are choosing mongodb to integrate into their ai products due to its unique vector search's ability to accurately scan text-based data that is also context aware. the vector search approach seems to allow more accurate results in an ai environment than traditional database queries. technology can change rapidly, but at present mongodb is gaining share as the preferred database provider for ai.
  • a price that costs a fraction of the historic database products also is a plus for adoption.
  • fiscal third-quarter (year ends january 31st) results were solid.
  • the company's core atlas product generated 30% vs. prior year revenue growth in the october quarter.
