What they do
Church & Dwight sells everyday staples like baking soda, detergent, condoms, oral care, and vitamins under brands you already know.
You buy these products on autopilot. That habit matters more than hype. Church & Dwight spends about 11% of sales on marketing and product work, and that keeps ARM & HAMMER, Trojan, Waterpik, and OxiClean in your cart. Walmart is 23% of 2025 sales, which is concentration risk, but it also proves these brands earn shelf space at brutal scale.

the number that mattered
Gross margin was 44.3%, which tells you brand power is still doing the heavy lifting even while revenue growth stays muted.
  • church & dwight closed 2025 on decent footing.
    even though the company has been facing a difficult market backdrop, ongoing business improvements helped bolster totals over the past few months. c&d exited the vitamins, minerals, and supplements (vms) business last year, and recently sold the flawless, spinbrush, and waterpik divisions.
  • it also acquired touchland in july.
  • the company invested in product innovation and marketing (spending roughly 11% of sales on these initiatives), which helped several of its brands gain market share.
    cost controls, better sourcing, and supply-chain improvements helped mitigate incremental tariff expenses and inflationary pressures.
  • plus, productivity enhancements and margin expansion bolstered profits.
  • in all, earnings per share rose 3% last year, while sales advanced 2%.
