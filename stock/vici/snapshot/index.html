What they do
VICI owns casino and entertainment real estate, then collects long-term rent from operators who do the gambling work.
VICI owns 54 gaming properties and 39 other experiential properties, or 93 cash-register locations tenants cannot casually pick up and move. Built-in escalators (contractual rent bumps → automatic annual rent increases → your rent check can rise without new deals) helped lift third-quarter 2025 revenue 4.4% to roughly $1.0 billion. You are betting on landlords with scarce assets, not on who gets lucky at blackjack.

the number that mattered
The key number was the -19.72% EPS miss, because a stock priced for dependable rent checks gets judged hardest when dependability slips.
  • vici properties posted a steady 2025 third quarter.
  • total revenue rose 4.4% from a year ago, to roughly $1.0 billion, driven by built-in escalators across the portfolio.
    funds from operations (ffo) totaled $0.71 per share, essentially unchanged vs. prior year, as the modest top-line growth was largely offset by higher interest expense and a higher share count. subsequent to the quarter’s end, vici announced the addition of its 14th tenant, clairvest, through a new long-term lease on mgm northfield park, which should improve tenant diversification.
  • the reit also raised its quarterly dividend to $0.45 per share, extending its streak of annual increases to eight years.
  • near-term prospects appear solid.
    management lifted its full-year outlook, implying mid-single-digit growth in ffo, despite limited reliance on external acquisitions.
  • roughly 90% of the rent roll carries escalation over time, linked to the consumer price index (cpi) and subject to caps, providing inflation protection.
    the northfield park lease is expected to close in the first half of 2026, which should help extend average lease duration and reinforce cash-flow visibility.
