What they do
Neogen sells food contamination tests and animal health products to farms, vets, and food processors.
Neogen wins because food and animal safety buyers hate changing vendors when audits are on the line. Food Safety is 71% of fiscal 2025 revenue, and those diagnostic kits sit inside your customers’ daily workflow. Switching costs (changing suppliers) → retraining, revalidating, and re-auditing → so what: once you are in, leaving is annoying and risky.

  • neogen delivered better-than-expected fiscal second-quarter results (period ended november 30th).
    the company reported adjusted earnings of $0.10 a share on revenues of $224.7 million, surpassing our estimates by $0.03 and about $15 million, respectively. much of the upside was generated within the food safety business, where performance benefited from higher demand for sample collection products and petrifilm. cost improvements and recently implemented strategic initiatives aimed at prioritizing higher-margin segments were also supportive of november-period comparisons.
  • leadership raised its full-year forecasts.
    neogen is now targeting fiscal 2026 revenues in the range of $845 million-$855 million and adjusted ebitda of approximately $175 million, versus its previous outlook of $820 million-$840 million and $165 million-$175 million. the revisions were motivated by the company's better-than-expected second-quarter results and accelerated progress across its transformation strategy. our current projections call for adjusted earnings of $0.30 a share in fiscal 2026, on revenues of $850 million.
  • we anticipate a return of top-line growth in fiscal 2027.
    recent revenue comparisons have been challenged by the impact of divestitures and discontinued product lines.
  • weaker volumes in certain end markets have also played a role.
    on a positive note, things should get a little easier next year, as impacts from divestitures and discontinuations lap and benefits from strategic initiatives gain further traction. operational improvements tied to the integration of petrifilm 20manufacturing and a renewed focus on higher-growth, solutions-based selling ought to provide additional firepower in fiscal 2027.

What could go wrong

the biggest risk here is a recovery story meeting hard reality: legal overhang, weak predictability, and a stock price already above the $8 long-term midpoint.

∘
med
ongoing litigation
The company faces multiple class-action lawsuits, including cases in the eastern district of pennsylvania and the southern district of new york. Legal noise is manageable until it starts consuming management attention and cash.
With $793M in long-term debt and a 9.8% net margin, Neogen does not have unlimited room for extra costs.
∘
med
the rebound may stay on paper
Fy2026 revenue estimates call for $850M against the current $434M base. If the business does not move toward that number, the recovery thesis starts looking like spreadsheet optimism.
That would pressure both the roughly 33x forward multiple and the $0.30 eps estimate at the same time.

multiple compression

The story works if margins recover fast. If they do not, 20% downside to $8 is already sitting in plain sight.
