What could go wrong

monday.com's main risk is not abstract competition. It's the combination of slower growth, thinner margins, and AI making the workflow layer easier to replicate.

∘
med
ai disintermediation
The fear is simple. If AI agents make it easier for teams to build basic workflow tools themselves, monday. Com's core value proposition gets weaker. That concern was part of the recent selloff because management is pitching AI as the growth answer while investors also see it as a substitution risk.
About $1.1B of the revenue shown on this page comes from subscriptions. If customers decide the workflow layer is easier to build than buy, the core engine takes the hit.
∘
med
margin compression
Gross margin was 90%, but management guided it to the mid-to-high 80s in 2026. Q4 gaap operating margin also fell to 1% from 4%.
That is a two-front problem: less profit on each revenue dollar and less confidence that scale is showing up where investors expected it to.
∘
med
valuation still assumes a cleaner story
A 105.6x trailing p/e is a rich multiple for a company guiding 18%–19% growth. The stock already dropped 20% after earnings, but expensive software rarely gets the benefit of the doubt when growth slips below 20%.
If the top line stays in the high teens and profitability does not recover, the business and the multiple can both disappoint at once.
∘
med
securities litigation overhang
A shareholder class action was filed on march 10, 2026, alleging securities fraud under sections 10(b) and 20(a) of the exchange act. Lawsuits do not always matter operationally, but they matter more when confidence is already fragile.
The direct financial cost is unclear in this snapshot. The near-term damage is attention, headlines, and one more reason for institutions to wait for cleaner execution.
If subscription growth slows while gross margin moves from 90% into the 80s, you do not just have a business problem. You have a valuation problem too.
