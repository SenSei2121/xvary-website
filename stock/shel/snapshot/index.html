the number that mattered
The number that mattered was the -0.7% EPS miss because Shell is already expected to be good at printing revenue. Investors wanted cleaner profit conversion.
  • shell reported decent results for the final stanza of 2025.
    amidst a challenging macroeconomic and crude oil pricing backdrop, the global energy operator registered only a modest vs. prior year sales contraction in the december period. meanwhile, on the earnings front, shell posted net income of about $1.42 per adr on the print used in this snapshot (just under the ~$1.43 consensus), which still represented a solid annual improvement versus softer prior periods. for the current year, we think shell is poised to deliver healthy vs. prior year double-digit top- and bottom-line advances.
  • shareholder-friendly policies are apt to remain in place, despite some industry peers scaling back.
    british rival bp recently stalled its buyback program due to rising debt concerns and pressured commodity prices.
  • however, shell appears set to move forward with consistent share repurchase activity.
    moreover, we think an increase to the quarterly dividend payout is imminent, which ought to keep the annual yield well ahead of the broader market average. the company’s energy portfolio is likely to continue to shift back toward traditional oil and gas operations. over the past year or so, management has been slowly trimming its clean energy assets, namely wind and alternative fuels projects. most recently, news surfaced that the company may be exploring the sale of sprng energy, its india-based solar and wind platform, further suggesting that leadership intends to reposition the operating portfolio.
  • while nothing concrete has been announced, there a number of potential buyers already in the queue.
    that said, while capital investments over the next few years ought to be heavily skewed toward downstream and upstream operations, we think shell is likely to take a more pragmatic approach to overall spending levels, with a keen focus on delivering value and a higher return on investment.
