What they do
Hawthorn is a community bank that takes deposits, makes loans, and sells trust and cash services across Missouri.
This is a local relationship business. Hawthorn had $1.9 billion in assets and 255 employees at December 31, 2025. If you run a small Missouri business, your loan, deposit account, and cash tools often sit in one place, and moving all of that is a hassle.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is Missouri commercial real estate credit deterioration.

!
high
Commercial Real Estate Concentration
You own a lender with concentrated commercial real estate exposure in one state. If Missouri property conditions weaken, credit costs can rise fast and local diversification does not bail you out.
This sits inside the core book that supports most of the bank's earnings power.
∘
med
Deposit Cost Pressure
About $78M of annual revenue comes from net interest income. That makes funding costs the main swing factor. If deposit competition heats up again, the recent margin improvement can reverse.
A spread squeeze hits the roughly 80% of revenue tied to interest income first.
∘
med
Thin Fee Income
Non-interest income was $20M and fell 8%. That matters because it is the cleaner, less rate-sensitive revenue bucket. When it shrinks, the bank leans harder on loan spreads.
Only about one-fifth of revenue comes from non-interest income, so diversification is limited.
~
low
Leadership Execution
The company announced a new CEO and President in April 2023. That is not fresh news now, but leadership choices still show up through credit mix, deposit pricing, and capital allocation.
The risk is less drama and more slow drift in underwriting discipline or returns.
About 80% of revenue comes from spread income, so this bank does not need a crisis to disappoint you. It just needs weaker credit, tighter margins, or both.
