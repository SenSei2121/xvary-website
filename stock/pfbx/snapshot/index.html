What they do
Peoples Financial runs a community bank on the Mississippi Gulf Coast, making money from loans, deposits, and basic banking services.
This bank wins the old-fashioned way. It has 17 branches and $846 million in assets on one coast it knows well. If you want a local lender for your business, real estate project, or municipal account, proximity still matters, and that local grip helps a 131-employee bank punch above its size.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is an activist proxy fight colliding with weaker profitability at a $97M Mississippi bank with only $23M of annual revenue.

∘
med
proxy contest and board turnover
The contested director election is set for 2026-04-22. If control shifts before operations improve, strategy changes can arrive faster than earnings repair.
The immediate risk is not theoretical. It has a date.
∘
med
falling profitability
Q2 2024 net income fell to $2.33M from $2.91M. When the business earns less and the stock still trades at 20.1x trailing earnings, the room for disappointment gets thin.
A $580K quarterly profit drop matters more when the whole company is worth $97M.
∘
med
rate sensitivity and revenue concentration
About $18.4M of the bank's $23M revenue comes from net interest income. That's 80% tied to the basic lending spread, which leaves limited diversification if deposit costs rise or loan yields stall.
One revenue engine means fewer places to hide.
∘
med
ownership concentration
Joseph Stilwell owns about 10% and kept buying, including 2,657 shares in 2026-03. Aligned owners can help, but one shareholder can also dominate the conversation in a thinly followed stock.
Influence is more concentrated here than it would be at a large regional bank.
With $23M of annual revenue, $18.4M tied to net interest income, and the latest quarter producing $2.33M of profit, PFBX does not have much room for a long board fight and sliding earnings at the same time.
