What they do
IDEX makes the small, critical parts that keep lab tools, fuel systems, and fire trucks working when failure is not an option.
IDEX wins by selling small parts that control expensive systems you cannot afford to shut down. Health and Science Technologies was 39.6% of 2024 sales, and international revenue was 50.4%, so you are not betting on one niche or one country. Switching costs (changing suppliers after your system is built in) → replacing validated parts inside critical equipment → so what: customers stay because downtime costs more than the part.

the number that mattered
The 28% growth in Health and Science matters most because that segment is already 39.6% of sales, so strength there moves the whole company.
  • idex corp. achieved double-digit top-line growth in the 2025 third quarter.
  • the manufacturer of various missioncritical products saw the strongest growth within its health & science technologies segment, reaching sales of $381 million.
  • the 28% vs. prior year growth for that operation was primarily driven by acquisition-related benefits of mott corp., which added filtration capabilities for high-purity applications.
  • meanwhile, the fluid & metering technologies division improved sales by 5%, over the same span, to $317.1 million.
  • but the fire & safety/diversified products business saw the top line retreat 3%, to $181.9 million.
    elsewhere, idex appears likely to have saved about $60 million in 2025 through streamlining operations.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is a fade in health & science growth once the Mott acquisition anniversary passes.

∘
med
acquisition-boosted growth normalizes
Health & Science Technologies hit $381M in latest-quarter sales, up 28% from a year ago. Management said Mott-related benefits helped. If that tailwind fades, the market loses its cleanest growth lane.
This matters because the fastest-growing segment is also the most obvious source of upside optimism in the current story.
∘
med
portfolio divergence gets worse
Fluid & Metering grew 5% to $317.1M while Fire & Safety / Diversified Products fell 3% to $181.9M. That is not one synchronized business. It is three demand cycles wearing one ticker.
When segment mix turns against you, consolidated revenue can still look fine while quality underneath gets weaker.
∘
med
premium multiple without premium returns
The stock trades at 22.7x trailing earnings with 11.0% return on capital and 5.8% revenue growth. That is a quality multiple for a business that is good, not unstoppable.
If growth slips or margins soften from 17.2%, the multiple has more room to compress than expand.
∘
med
institutions keep reducing exposure
Last quarter showed 280 institutional buyers against 320 sellers, with 75.9M shares still held overall. That is not panic, but it is not support either.
A stock can be fundamentally fine and still go nowhere if the marginal buyer keeps walking past it.
A 17.2% margin business with one segment up 28%, one up 5%, and one down 3% is sturdy, but it is not simple. The risk is that the market stops paying 22.7x earnings for a portfolio that looks more mixed than premium.
