The number that mattered was the 116.67% EPS surprise, because it shows how violently this business can outperform when refining conditions line up.
  • shares of par pacific have been on a rollercoaster ride over the past three months.
    the stock appreciated strongly over the course of 2025, etching an alltime high water mark in november of last year. to start 2026, the equity dipped sharply, largely due to pressured oil prices and geopolitical concerns, but quickly recovered back to all-time high territory.
  • that said, we are not recommending the stock for short-term-minded accounts at this juncture.
    par pacific shares are only ranked to mirror the broader market averages over the coming six to 12 months (timeliness: 3).
  • sales ought to rebound this year, while share earnings should be respectable.
    the domestic petroleum refiner probably reported a slight top-line contraction for the full-year 2025, due partly to a tepid commodity pricing backdrop. but we think 2026 holds more promise, and should be led by strength from the retail segment and healthier consumer energy demand. the bottom line should continue to benefit from small refinery exemptions stemming from recently positive legislation. lastly, resilient refining margins and the potential for more-supportive crude oil prices underpins our relatively optimistic stance. despite the absence of a dividend payment, the company is returning a decent amount of capital to shareholders. since 2023, the company has been aggressively buying back stock, with $16 million spent in the third quarter of 2025. based on the current authorization, we think periodic share repurchases are apt to continue through the end of this decade.
  • an upcoming project in hawaii is encouraging.
    as previously noted, the renewable fuels joint venture with mitsubishi and eneos is expected to be operational in the near term. the facility is slated to produce up to 2,400 barrels per day of sustainable aviation fuel and renewable diesel.
  • at the moment, the stock does not stand out for the long haul, either.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is hawaii refining margins rolling over.

!
high
crack spread compression
This is the core risk. Refiners make money on the gap between crude costs and refined product prices. If that spread narrows, revenue can stay large while profit disappears fast.
This risk sits under essentially the full $7.5B revenue stream and attacks the 2.8% net margin directly.
∘
med
island logistics and supply disruption
The hawaii footprint helps competitively, but it also concentrates operational risk. If supply chains get hit or local operating conditions change, the same geography that helps the moat can hurt results.
A business with $962M of long-term debt and 20 / 100 price stability does not have much room for operational sloppiness.
∘
med
renewable project execution
The hawaii renewable fuels joint venture is supposed to add up to 2,400 barrels per day of sustainable aviation fuel and renewable diesel. Delays, cost overruns, or weak economics would keep PARR tied even more tightly to its legacy refining cycle.
The project is strategically interesting because the existing business only converts 2.8% of revenue into profit.
~
low
the low-multiple trap
A 6.7x trailing P/E looks cheap. But with earnings predictability at 5 / 100 and a 3–5 year midpoint target of $35, the market may simply be treating a cyclical earner like a cyclical earner.
If FY2026 EPS misses the $5.05 estimate, the valuation argument gets thinner in a hurry.
The business generated $7.5B in revenue last year, but the real exposure is margin: at a 2.8% net margin, small changes in refining economics have outsized effects on earnings.
