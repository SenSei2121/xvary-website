What just happened
beat estimates
Allegro's last reported quarter showed $0.15 EPS, ahead of the $0.13 estimate.
The beat came as the company continued to climb out of a weak period in demand. Revenue reached about $229.2M in fiscal Q3 2026, while gross margin improved to 49.9% in the company release.

the number that mattered
The key number was 49.9% gross margin, because it suggests the recovery is not just about more units shipped but better economics per chip.
  • allegro microsystems is seeing a meaningful recovery in financial results.
  • fiscal 2025 third-quarter sales increased 29% from a year ago. (year ends march 27th.) bookings and the backlog improved with solid demand schedules for automotive e-mobility, adas (advanced driver assistance systems), and data centers.
    despite depressed automotive manufacturing volumes, the adoption of hybrid vehicles and adas solutions supported segment growth. meanwhile, higher manufacturing utilization tied to volume growth supported a strong operating margin. this was partially offset by a higher mix of china sales, which have been pressured by tariff activity.
  • the automotive segment is a core business for long-term growth.
    it has been the largest growth pillar for allegro in recent years, and management expects this to persist. the higher number of average system solutions per vehicle has supported this expansion in sales, despite industry headwinds.
  • there is a runway for rapid expansion should vehicle volumes make a recovery.
    however, customer production sites are running on lean inventory levels, indicating near-term difficulties. the tensions around a trade war have weighed on relationships with chinabased electric vehicle brands nio and byd.
  • the data center industry is a second long-term growth opportunity for allegro.
