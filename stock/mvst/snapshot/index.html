What they do
Microvast makes batteries and battery parts for electric trucks and utility-scale storage sites.
It builds the battery from cell materials to software controls. Vertical integration means fewer outside vendors can delay your pack. The company also has a 53.5Ah NMC cell built for fast charging and long life.

What could go wrong

the risk is not abstract. MVST is trying to prove a low-margin manufacturing business can scale cleanly while trade exposure, policy shifts, and write-down risk all sit on the table at the same time.

∘
med
china-linked manufacturing and tariff exposure
MVST is a U.S.-listed company with China-linked operations. Tariffs, trade restrictions, or procurement scrutiny can hit costs, customer appetite, or both.
Policy shifts already matter because South Korean changes affect roughly 30% of ESS revenue. Thin margins do not give you much room to absorb another policy shock.
∘
med
persistent unprofitability
Q4 2025 EPS was -$0.11. Gross margin was 36.6%, but operating margin was only 1.4%. That gap tells you overhead, scale inefficiency, or both are still the story.
Record revenue without margin conversion does not prove the model. It just proves the company can stay busy while shareholders wait.
∘
med
impairment and inventory write-down risk
Recent results were hit by impairment charges. When a hardware company takes write-downs, accounting is telling you asset values or demand assumptions overshot reality.
Even modest write-downs can erase a 1.4% operating margin fast. The lower the cushion, the louder the write-off.
∘
med
execution risk on the 2026 production ramp
Management is leaning on 2026 expansion plans. In batteries, higher volume helps only when pricing, manufacturing yields, and cost discipline improve with it.
With price stability at 5 / 100, the stock usually reacts first and reads the details later. Miss the ramp and you will feel it quickly.
Between roughly 30% ESS policy exposure, a 1.4% operating margin, and q4 EPS of -$0.11, MVST does not have much room for execution mistakes. That's the whole risk case in one sentence.
