the number that mattered
The 6% revenue drop mattered most because this is still a scale business: flat margins help, but you need orders to keep earnings growing.
  • lindsay corporation opened fiscal 2026 on a down note. (year ends august 31st.) total sales for the manufacturer of irrigation and infrastructure equipment decreased 6% from a year ago, to $155.8 million, primarily due to a 9% slide in the irrigation segment as north american farmers grappled with low commodity prices and high input costs.
    still, the company defended its profitability, through aggressive price management and cost-containment measures, leading to an improved gross margin in the irrigation unit that muted the impact of softer demand. management expects north america irrigation market conditions to remain soft in the near term until growers gain further trade certainty and see improvement in commodity prices that support net farm income.
  • conversely, increased road construction activity supported a good start to the fiscal year for the infrastructure segment, which delivered a 17% increase in sales compared to the same year-ago period.
    moving forward, lindsay faces a difficult fiscal second-quarter revenue comparison in infrastructure, as a $20 million road zipper system project in the prior-year will not be replaced. however, the company anticipates increased road zipper leasing and higher sales of road safety products to offset approximately half of the revenue loss throughout the year. for the remainder of fiscal 2026, the company is pivoting to capture international momentum to offset domestic sluggishness. this move underpins lindsay’s dual-pronged strategy of international expansion to capture emerging demand and infrastructure innovation to drive higher-margin, tech-based growth.
  • recent initiatives include partnerships with regional governments and development agencies promoting irrigation modernization projects, in india and africa, where mechanized irrigation penetration remains below 20%.
  • investors with a 3 to 5-year stance may want to consider the stock.
    lindsay is a long-cycle, structural growth opportunity, supported by infrastructure modernization as well as food and water scarcity.
  • due to this, our model suggests shares of lnn offer solid capital gains prospects out to 2029-2031.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is an irrigation demand slowdown after a 29.2% growth year.

∘
med
growth normalization in irrigation
last year delivered 29.2% revenue growth. this year is estimated at $660M versus $659M last year. that is a hard deceleration.
If flat revenue becomes the new normal, investors stop paying for a growth story and start valuing a cyclical equipment maker at 18.3x trailing earnings.
∘
med
project timing in infrastructure
management already flagged a tougher comparison because a prior $20M project will not repeat next quarter. small companies feel lumpiness faster.
When a single project moves, reported growth moves with it. You can get a weak-looking quarter even without a broken long-term thesis.
∘
med
margin defense stops working
the latest quarter held up because price management and cost containment offset softer sales. that is useful, but it is not infinitely repeatable.
A business earning a 10.7% net margin has room for error, not room for complacency. if price and cost actions fade, EPS pressure follows quickly.
∘
med
data thinness on segment mix
this snapshot does not provide a clean irrigation-versus-infrastructure revenue split, which means you are partly underwriting management commentary rather than a fully segmented model.
Less segment detail means less precision. when a company is only about $1B in market cap, that matters more than it does in a megacap.
Flat revenue at about $660M plus any slippage from the current 10.7% net margin would leave very little room for multiple expansion.
