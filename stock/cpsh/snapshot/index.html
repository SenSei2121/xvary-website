What they do
CPS makes specialized metal-and-ceramic parts and lightweight armor for trains, EVs, internet hardware, wind systems, and defense gear.
CPS sells metal matrix composites, or MMCs. MMCs → metal mixed with ceramics → parts that run cooler, lighter, and tougher. That matters when your customer is building EVs, wind systems, or military gear and failure is expensive. The whole company has just 92 employees, yet it sells into six end markets listed by the company. Small headcount, specialized parts, hard jobs.

What could go wrong

CPSH's risk is not abstract. A $33M revenue base, a 3.2% operating margin, and $19M tied to defense & aerospace means a few customer programs do a lot of the heavy lifting.

!
high
Program concentration
Defense & aerospace generated $19M, or 58% of revenue. When one end market carries that much weight, a delayed order or canceled program hits hard.
A disruption here goes straight at the biggest part of the $33M revenue base.

high
Thin margins
Gross margin was 16.7% and operating margin was 3.2%. That means a modest jump in input costs, scrap, or labor can erase profit quickly.
The company kept only 3.2 cents of operating profit on each revenue dollar last year.
