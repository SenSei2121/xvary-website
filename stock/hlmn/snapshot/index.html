the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $1.2B in revenue, because it shows the quarter was big even if the market still wanted more.
  • hillman’s 2025 fourth-quarter results were in line with our expectations.
  • sales rose 4.5%, to $365 million, in the december period, capping a record 2025 with $1.55 billion in sales and net income over $40 million.
  • the backdrop was still choppy, with u.s. existing-home sales totalling just under 4.1 million in 2025, one of the weakest levels in decades, and mortgage rates have stayed near 6%, which tends to dampen move-related home improvement demand.
  • market volumes fell about 5% in 2025, meaning growth leaned more heavily on tariff-driven pricing and share gains rather than a healthy end market.
  • housing-linked demand is still a headwind, but the decade could end stronger.
