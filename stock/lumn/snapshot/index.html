What they do
Lumen sells broadband, voice, fiber networking, cloud, and TV services to homes, businesses, and government customers.
Lumen still matters because it owns a large fiber and telecom footprint in a business where ripping out your network is painful and expensive. It is the third-largest telephone company in the U.S. and employs about 29,000 people, which tells you this is infrastructure, not an app. If your company runs on its network, switching vendors means moving the digital plumbing that keeps offices, calls, and data running.

the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $0.23 EPS because Wall Street expected a loss, and surprise beats are one reason the stock nearly doubled in the last 12 months.
  • lumen technologies completed the sale of its fiber-to-the-home business to at&t.
    on february 2nd, lumen took a major step in its lengthy business transformation strategy with a focus on shoring up its balance sheet through the sale of its fiber assets for $5.75 billion in cash. the transaction allows the company to focus on being a pure play enterprise-focused technology infrastructure company that emphasizes ai connectivity opportunities.
  • proceeds of $4.8 billion from the transaction will be used to retire debt and lower annual interest costs by roughly $300 million.
    the company plans to ramp up investments in its core fiber backbone network infrastructure to support ai and advanced cloud workloads.
  • these shares have perked up some lately.
  • the stock price has increased another 9% over the last three months and almost doubled in price in the last twelve months.
    while the aforementioned divestiture contributed to investors’ enthusiasm, so did news that the company received an ai governance certification, further validating key innovations.
  • also, investors took notice of ceo kate johnson’s purchase of $500,000 worth of lumn common stock.
    despite a dismal bottom-line performance on the backdrop of an unfavorable top-line mix in 2025, recent developments and december-quarter results point to encouraging signs on the horizon. the company has secured multi-year ai infrastructure orders worth roughly $13 billion for its private connectivity fiber deployments, thereby reinforcing expectations of bottom-line improvement.
