What they do
Ondas sells private wireless gear and autonomous drones to rail, utility, defense, and government customers.
This company sells into places where failure is expensive. It has 802.16s private wireless tech and multi-patented radios for railroads, utilities, and government networks, plus drone systems for critical infrastructure. With just 113 employees, you are betting a very small team can become embedded in mission-critical jobs where ripping out equipment is painful.

What could go wrong

The #1 risk is missing the stepped-up fiscal 2026 revenue target (management raised outlook to on the order of $375M after 2025 results).

∘
med
Revenue ramp misses the promise
The stock is framed around a jump from ~$51M in 2025 revenue to a much larger 2026 target. If that target is cut or slips, you are no longer debating timing—you are debating the entire setup.
Impact: the valuation story loses its anchor because current revenue is too small to support a $5B market cap on its own.
∘
med
Losses stay extreme
A -176.1% operating margin means the company loses more than it sells. If revenue growth arrives slower than expected, persistent losses and capital needs move from background issue to main event.
Impact: equity holders face a business still dependent on future scale while current operating economics remain deeply negative.
∘
med
Execution gets split across too many fronts
Private wireless, drone systems, defense partnerships, and Rotron Aerospace integration all have to translate into commercial traction. For a company with ~$51M in annual revenue, that is still a lot of moving pieces.
Impact: even good technology can get buried by complexity if customers, integration, and scaling do not line up at the same time.
At billions of market value against ~$51M of 2025 revenue, this stock leaves very little room for a slow ramp.
