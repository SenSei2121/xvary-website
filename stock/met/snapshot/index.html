What just happened
missed estimates
MetLife's last reported quarter delivered a 50.8% EPS miss, with $1.17 reported against a $2.38 estimate.
Revenue was $1.8B, up 196% vs. prior year in the SEC data. The weird part is the data conflict: the quarterly history table shows 2025 fourth-quarter EPS of $2.38, while consensus marks the latest report at $1.17.

the number that mattered
The 50.8% miss matters most because a 9.5x stock only stays cheap-good if you can trust the earnings number.
  • metlife's profit comparisons strengthened in the third quarter.
    although top-line growth has remained modest throughout 2025, narrower benefits payments and favorable investment returns drove a bottom line that advanced by double digits sequentially and relative to the prior year. thus, the figure of $2.34 per share outpaced our $2.20 estimate and the street's more demanding $2.32.
  • with these trends likely to remain in place through year-end, we raised our share-earnings view for the current quarter to $2.38 from $2.22 previously. Improved investment income is offsetting more modest premiums.
  • the vast majority of metlife's managed assets are invested in fixed income instruments, with close to 70% of that in high-quality securities rated A or higher by major ratings agencies.
  • despite efforts by the federal reserve over the past year to lower short-term interest rates, yields on these long-term holdings continued to trend higher through the september period.
  • in fact, the weighted average yield for the recent interim climbed by nearly 25 basis points from last year, to 4.99%.
    as the asset management arms of metlife have steadily shifted away from mortgage loans (especially within the challenged commercial segment), it has added to positions within the broad bond category, including private placements.
