What they do
Flux sells lithium-ion battery systems that replace old lead-acid packs in forklifts and airport ground equipment.
Flux wins by fitting into messy fleet operations, not by being the biggest. Its battery packs are modular (modular → mix-and-match designs → one platform can fit different equipment), and its wireless battery system gives real-time monitoring (battery management system → live pack health data → fewer surprises for your fleet). A 101-person company produced $66 million of annual revenue, or about $653,000 per employee, and it just added more than $3.6 million of airline purchase orders.
