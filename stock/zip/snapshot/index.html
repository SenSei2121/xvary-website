the number that mattered
The $0.31 EPS beat mattered most, because it says ZipRecruiter can still squeeze profit out of a weak hiring market.
  • ziprecruiter registered a per-share loss in the third quarter.
    the company posted a wider deficit of $0.11, compared with $0.03 in the year-ago period, reflecting higher sales and marketing spending.
  • meanwhile, revenue increased slightly vs. prior year, to $115 million, due to slow hiring activity.
  • a closer look at the results shows that while quarterly paid employers grew at a low single-digit pace over the last year, revenue per paid employer fell at a similar rate.
    this indicates ongoing softness in hiring demand, especially among its core client base of small and medium-sized businesses (smbs).
  • near-term headwinds will likely persist, but improvement still seems possible.
    the company will probably continue to navigate an uncertain macro backdrop, and visibility for the fourth quarter remains limited due to seasonality.
  • as a result, we estimate a $0.55 per-share deficit in 2025.
