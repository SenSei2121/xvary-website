What they do
It is a community bank that takes deposits, makes loans, and sells title services across Ohio, Indiana, and Michigan.
This bank wins the old-fashioned way: it is already in your town. It has 27 offices, 27 ATMs, 6 loan production offices, and 224 employees across Ohio, Indiana, and Michigan. Banking relationships are switching costs (switching costs → the hassle of moving your money and loans → so what: customers often stay put), and that matters more when your lender also handles small business, farm, mortgage, and trust work under one roof.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is revenue shrinkage at a small community bank priced as a steady income stock.

∘
med
forecast revenue decline
analysts expect revenue to fall 10.9% a year. that is the opposite of what you want in a bank where the dividend and valuation both depend on steady earnings power.
if that decline shows up in reported numbers, the 9.9x p/e stops looking cheap and starts looking accurate.
∘
med
rate sensitivity in the lending book
net interest income is $12.7M in the disclosed mix. if loan yields compress faster than deposit costs, the spread business gets squeezed.
that pressure hits earnings quality first, then valuation.
∘
med
thin operating cushion
a B balance sheet and $65M of long-term debt, or 34% of capital, leave less room for mistakes than a top-tier regional bank would have.
if credit costs rise or revenue disappoints again, the margin story gets tested fast.
∘
med
small-cap liquidity and sentiment
zacks downgraded the stock and short interest increased 21.8% in february 2026. for a $125M company, that can weigh on price action even when the quarter is merely mixed.
this risk does not break the business. it does raise the odds that the stock moves harder than the fundamentals justify.
if revenue keeps falling at the forecast 10.9% pace, your 3.1% yield starts looking less like income and more like compensation for owning a shrinking bank.
