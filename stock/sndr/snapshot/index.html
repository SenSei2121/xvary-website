the number that mattered
The 8-cent EPS miss matters because it says volume did not turn into enough profit.
  • schneider national closed out 2025 on a weak note.
    earnings came in at $0.13 a share in the fourth quarter, below consensus expectations of around $0.20.
  • despite higher revenues (+5% from a year ago), the bottom line faltered versus the december period of 2024.
    performance softened mainly due to a severely truncated peak season beginning in november, as shippers aggressively drew down inventories rather than restocking for holiday demand. unplanned automotive production shutdowns with key customers disrupted dedicated operations throughout november. simultaneously, spiking third-party carrier costs compressed logistics margins, while elevated healthcare expenses added unexpected cost pressure. what's more, poor midwest weather compounded volume challenges, though tighter capacity in late-december provided brief relief, as regulatory-related competitor exits were absorbed by remaining carriers.
  • however, the outlook for 2026 is much rosier.
    while performance improvement likely hinges on accelerating capacity exits from heightened regulatory enforcement, management's estimate of $40 million in savings from workforce reductions and ai-related productivity enhancements is encouraging.
  • on the demand side, catalysts include stronger tax refunds, interest rate cuts, and federal infrastructure investments that could trigger inventory restocking following aggressive year-end drawdowns.
    we believe these factors will allow earnings to bounce back to $1.00 a share, on revenues of $5.9 billion.
  • the next 3 to 5 years hold promise.
    sustained growth from multi-year rate restoration is likely, as constrained capacity strengthens pricing.

What could go wrong

the top risk is freight pricing and demand softness in north american trucking.

freight pricing rolls over
this is a low-margin freight operator. if pricing weakens, the impact shows up quickly because there is no large high-margin segment to offset it.
with a 3.6% net margin and 1.8% margin in the latest quarter, even a small rate miss can hit earnings hard.

the recovery gets pushed out
