What they do
Avnet buys electronic parts in bulk and sells them to companies building hardware.
Avnet is not a brand play. It is the plumbing. You get 14,869 employees moving inventory, shipping, and credit across 94% Electronic Components and 6% Farnell sales. the number that mattered
The $1.05 EPS beat the $0.75 estimate by 40%, which shows Avnet can still squeeze profit out of a 10.5% gross margin.
  • shares of avnet have advanced more than 30% in price since our december review.
    this move likely reflects the company’s return to double-digit earnings growth in the second quarter of fiscal 2026, following elevated customer inventory levels and weak business conditions in the past two years.
  • on an apples-toapples basis, adjusted share earnings of $1.05 compared favorably with the $0.87 tally (see footnote b) registered in the year-ago period, while sales advanced 12% and came in ahead of management’s guidance.
  • the bulk of these gains can be attributed to an 11% sales advance in the core electronics components business (93% of total) thanks to strong spending in data center and ai-related programs.
    in particular, the company saw a pickup in compute orders after a previous slowdown, coupled with strengthening demand in the aerospace and defense arm.
  • meanwhile, last year’s restructuring efforts at the smaller farnell business supported better margins and profitability, as well as a 24% jump in sales.
    this was driven by new product introductions and a continued recovery in demand for on-board components (namely in europe), with support from cross-selling initiatives.
  • we look for a strong top- and bottom-line recovery in fiscal 2026.
    avnet finished the quarter with an above-parity book-to-bill ratio (above 1.0 means new orders are coming in faster than products are being shipped out), indicating a positive sign for future revenue. it received $150 million of high-demand memory and storage inventory at the end of the quarter that was converted into revenue in january. we think avnet will continue to come out of a slump with our projection of double-digit sales and earnings gains this year. higher growth in the americas and emea regions should be met with a more gradual uptick in asia, as customers renew investments in electronics and infrastructure.

What could go wrong

the top threat is the $268M legal award hitting a business with only 1.9% net margins.

∘
med
legal award economics
$268M is not small when annual revenue is $22.2B but profit margin is just 1.9%. In a higher-margin business, you absorb that. Here, you feel it.
Based on the current margin, the award equals roughly 64% of one year's net income.
∘
med
inventory and demand whiplash
This company just lived through elevated customer inventories and weak conditions, and its earnings predictability score is only 45/100. That is analyst-speak for a business that can surprise you in the wrong direction.
Full-year EPS fell from $5.43 to $2.75 before the current recovery started to show up.
∘
med
margin stays too thin
Latest quarterly margin was 1.1%. Even if revenue improves, tiny margins leave little room for freight costs, pricing pressure, or execution misses.
A business keeping one to two cents of each sales dollar does not need a disaster to disappoint shareholders.
a legal hit this size does not break a $22.2B company, but it matters a lot more when the business only earns about $422M a year at a 1.9% net margin.
