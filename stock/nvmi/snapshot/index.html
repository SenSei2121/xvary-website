Nova sells chip-factory tools that measure film thickness and tiny wafer features before defects turn expensive.

the number that mattered
The $433M quarter matters because it came in 45% above last year.
  • at the end of last year, revenue rose to $222.6 million, which marked 14% vs. prior year growth.
    this achievement highlighted the company’s robust portfolio, solid customer demand, and disciplined operational focus.
  • for the quarter, approximately 75% of revenue stemmed from logic and foundry and 25% from memory.
  • the company reported blended gross margins of 59.6% on a non-gaap basis, which was at the upper end of its target range of 57% to 60%.
    operating expenses increased to $62 million on a non-gaap basis, reflecting targeted investments in r&d and sales and marketing to advance the product roadmap and drive future growth.
  • operating margins were strong, reaching 32% on a non-gaap basis.
  • what’s more, earnings per share rose to $2.14.
