What they do
Nephros sells water filters and pathogen testing systems to hospitals, dialysis centers, and commercial buildings.
Nephros does the unglamorous part of healthcare: keeping bacteria and viruses out of water. That matters because a bad shower, sink, or dialysis line can become a medical problem fast. With just 31 employees, the company still produced a 63.0% gross margin in the latest filing, which tells you customers pay up for filtration that works.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is tariff pressure on Italian-sourced filtration products.

tariff pressure on imported products
A 10% tariff on imports from Italy cut Q4 gross margin to 58% from 64%. For a business earning roughly $0.01 per share, that kind of squeeze is not background noise — it is the story.
impact: weaker gross profit on the company’s main revenue line

growth slowdown in a one-engine business
Revenue grew 33% last year, which is impressive. It also carries the valuation. If that slows meaningfully while margins stay pressured, a 30.8x trailing P/E stops looking like optimism and starts looking like wishful thinking.
impact: multiple compression and lower confidence in the growth case
∘
med
thin margin for error on profitability
This is a near-break-even company with a C++ balance sheet, 5/100 price stability, and only $1M in long-term debt. Low debt helps. Thin earnings do not. One weak quarter can move the narrative fast.
impact: volatility, capital raise risk, or both if execution slips
