What they do
InterGroup owns one big hotel, a small apartment portfolio, and an investment book that can swing reported results.
You cannot casually recreate a 558-room hotel with a ballroom, underground garage, and pedestrian bridge in Los Angeles. InterGroup also owns 16 apartment complexes, three houses, one commercial property, and 2 acres in Maui. Hard assets are the moat here: real estate you can touch, in markets where replacing it costs more and takes longer than most rivals can handle.

What could go wrong

INTG does not need a dramatic macro disaster to get in trouble. the filings already give you the problem set: a going concern warning, weak controls, and $193M of debt against a $78M market cap.
