the number that mattered
The $1.41 EPS number matters because it shows the quarter was not just bigger, it was far more profitable.
  • antero resources has joined other competitors in pursuing strategic transactions.
    late in 2025, the company entered into an agreement to acquire the upstream assets of hg energy ii for $2.8 billion in cash plus the assumption of hg energy’s commodity hedge book.
  • simultaneously, antero announced the sale of its ohio utica shale acreage for $800 million in cash.
    the pending purchase (if approved) would add 385,000 net acres that have the space for over 400 wells, producing 850 million cubic feet equivalent (mmcfe) per day. furthermore, while the assets would add to antero’s inventory of wells, they are also expected to help improve the company’s overall cost structure. management is looking for planning optimization and drilling & completions savings, as well as reduced intangible costs, to generate $950 million in during the next 10 years.
  • that said, the divestiture of the utica acreage is estimated to remove 150 mmcfe from volume.
    management expects hg energy’s holdings to be accretive to free cash flow upon closing, while the non-core utica assets divestiture should help to reduce debt further.
  • the production picture for 2026 is coming into focus.
    the development of existing assets, along with prior acquisitions (that aggregate approximately $260 million), position the company to boost output, pending favorable market prices. antero intends to maintain the likely 2025 fourth-quarter volume rate of 3.25 to 3.5 billion cubic feet equivalent (bcfe) at legacy holdings this year.
  • but the addition of hg energy’s assets would lift output to 4.2 to 4.225 bcfe.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is integrating the $2.8B HG Energy II acquisition while completing the $800M Utica sale.

∘
med
deal execution risk
AR is trying to close a $2.8B purchase, assume a hedge book, and sell other acreage for $800M in the same window. This is not routine housekeeping. It is a full capital-allocation test.
If the acquisition does not become accretive to free cash flow, the 2026 rerating case weakens fast.
∘
med
volume replacement math
The Utica divestiture removes 150 mmcfe of volume. HG Energy II is supposed to offset that with 850 mmcfe per day plus better inventory quality. That is the numerical logic behind the deal.
If output does not move from the 3.25–3.5 bcfe legacy range toward 4.2–4.225 bcfe, investors will question the whole transaction logic.
∘
med
commodity price sensitivity
All $5.3B of revenue comes from selling produced volumes. There is no second business line to smooth a weak gas market, and the 15/100 earnings-predictability score says exactly that.
Low gas prices would pressure revenue, margins, and the market's patience at the same time.
Miss the close, miss the production ramp, or miss the promised $950M of 10-year savings and the $56 midpoint starts to look like spreadsheet optimism. You own a cleaner-balance-sheet producer, not a low-drama one.
