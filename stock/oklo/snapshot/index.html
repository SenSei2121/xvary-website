  • oklo continues to make strides toward advancing its fast fission nuclear technology.
  • last year, oklo and its subsidiary, atomic alchemy, were selected by the u.s.
  • department of energy (doe) for three of eleven awarded reactor pilot programs (rpps).
    the most notable of these is the aurora-inl (idaho national laboratory) powerhouse, a 75 megawatts of electricity (mwe), liquid metal-cooled fast reactor. the aurora-inl powerhouse is designed to generate clean and scalable energy for industrial sites, data centers and communities through a modular design that offers the ability to utilize recycled fuel. oklo is actively coordinating with the doe to pursue authorization that can accelerate the timeline to initial operations. to that end, the doe has recently approved the preliminary documented safety analysis (pdsa) for the aurora fuel fabrication facility, marking the start of its assembly. the pdsa represents the second of three safety-basis documents to be approved for authorization, with the next step being an updated documented safety analysis (dsa) based on final design and construction once the facility is completed. oklo and meta platforms, inc. (nasdaq: meta) have announced an agreement that supports significant nuclear energy development. oklo plans to develop a 1.2 gigawatt power campus in pike county, ohio, to support meta’s data centers in the region. the agreement allows meta to prepay for power while providing funding to lay the foundation for oklo’s construction of multiple aurora powerhouses on 206 acres of company-owned land purchased from the doe. pre-construction is slated for this year, with the first phase targeted to come online in 2030, expanding incrementally to deliver the full target of 1.2 gw by 2034.
