the number that mattered
EPS of $1.29 missed the $1.40 estimate by 7.86%, which says cost control, not demand, is the issue.
  • pricesmart started fiscal year 2026 on solid footing (year ends august 31st).
  • the company reported comparable net merchandise sales up 7.2% (6.5% in constant currency) through the first eight weeks of the november quarter.
    during that period, the elera point-of-sale system rollout was completed across englishspeaking caribbean markets, and the initial implementation of workday’s human capital management platform began. operations have already started at a new dry distribution center in guatemala, and the panama facility has been adapted to handle cold merchandise. management’s anticipated ‘‘year of growth’’ for fiscal 2026 hinges on aggressive physical and operational expansion. three new clubs are scheduled to open: la romana, dominican republic, in spring 2026, montego bay, jamaica, in summer 2026, and kingston, jamaica, in fall 2026. the company is also advancing its entry into chile, having hired a general manager and secured a club site. supply chain investments include new companyrun distribution centers in trinidad and the dominican republic.
  • these should reduce lead times and lower total ‘‘door-todoor’’ product sourcing costs.
  • further, the migration to the relex forecasting system should improve in-stock availability.
  • digital enhancements include an upgraded mobile app for better performance on iphone and android devices, along with expanded benefits for high-value platinum membership offerings.
    several financial headwinds temper this optimistic outlook and warrant monitoring throughout the fiscal year. general and administrative expenses will rise as the company resumes ceo compensation following an interim period without such costs. currency liquidity remains challenging, particularly in honduras, due to strict controls and in trinidad, where the company held nearly $60 million in local currency as of august 31, 2025. management also acknowledges potential risks from changes in u.s. remittance flows to key central american markets, though consumption impacts have not yet materialized.
