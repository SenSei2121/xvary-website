Robert Half helps companies hire accountants, admins, legal staff, and tech workers, then sells consulting through Protiviti.

  • robert half will probably end the year on a dim note, as the job market has continued to cool.
    for much of 2025, the staffer’s top and bottom lines have been down, vs. prior year, with demand for temp- and permanent-placement professionals in retreat.
  • on the nonstaffing consulting side of business, protiviti began drifting lower by the third quarter, as well.
    it seems inflation and macroeconomic uncertainty has continued to weigh on both clients and candidates, causing them to remain cautious.
  • this has led to subdued hiring and new project activity.
    yet, management noted encouraging revenue trends in contract talent solutions, with sequential growth in september and into october.
  • still, we think fourth-quarter results will be down, with full-year share earnings being nearly halved, to $1.30, on a revenue slip of 7%.
  • despite early signs of a pickup, we have lowered the bar for 2026 on the hazy labor market picture.
    increasing client discussions about deferred projects and hiring for critical jobs is surely good news. but job-placement activity will likely remain weak near term, given concerns over a potential slowdown in the economy amid ongoing macro uncertainty.
