the number that mattered
94.7% means the company spent $94.70 on claims and expenses for every $100 of premiums. Lower is better, and 94.7% is not a blowout.
  • old republic international registered a decent earnings improvement for full-year 2025.
  • the insurer benefited from a more-than-10% increase in net premiums earned for the full year, due to new business wins and healthy price increases on existing business.
  • the company also benefited from a decent increase in net investment income for the year.
    though this line item has been pressured to an extent by reduced bond reinvestment rates, a higher level of invested assets has provided support.
  • these positive factors helped to combat a higher combined ratio for the year (lower is better), though the underwriting margin remained in positive territory.
  • we look for the story to remain largely the same in each of the next two years.
    the broader insurance market has been in a secular upswing for quite some time and we believe that it has some runway left despite some headwinds. net premiums earned ought to continue to trend higher, thanks to increased new business opportunities and favorable pricing trends. investment income is worth keeping a close eye on, however, given the aforementioned bond-reinvestment-rate pressures.
