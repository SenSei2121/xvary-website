missing quarter
The most important number is missing because this is a fund, not an operating company with clean quarterly sales.
  • the mexico fund remains on a roll.
  • fiscal 2025 was one of the fund’s best years in the past two decades, with returns based on net asset value (nav) exceeding 30%. (year ends on october 31st.) moreover, a sharp contraction in the discount to nav helped to push share-price returns north of 45%.
    on the downside, the fund’s underweighting in some of mexico’s better performing equities, including industrias penoles (mining), america movil (telecom services), and cemex (building materials), caused nav-based returns to lag behind the broader mexican market.
  • since the start of fiscal 2026, the discount has expanded slightly, but this was more than offset during the january quarter by a 12% increase in nav.
  • the fund is returning more cash to shareholders.
  • last month, the quarterly distribution was hiked 40%, to $0.35 a share.
    this represents the second increase in the payout in less than a year, and follows last summer’s news that saba capital had established a sizable stake in the fund. saba is a prominent investor in closed end funds and has a history of pursuing an activist approach to reduce the discount to nav.

the #1 risk here is mexico market exposure packed inside a small closed-end fund. if mexican equities stumble, there is no operating business underneath to bail you out.
