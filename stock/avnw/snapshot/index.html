What could go wrong

the #1 risk is fiber replacing microwave on the routes that matter most.

∘
med
fiber keeps taking the easy economics
Microwave wins when fiber is impractical. Fiber wins when customers want more capacity and can justify the build. That directly pressures the product side of the business.
Product sales are roughly ~69% of the revenue shown on this page. That's where the pressure would land first.
∘
med
thin margins leave little room for mistakes
A 32.8% gross margin and 1.3% return on capital are not disaster numbers. They are also not the kind of numbers that forgive execution slips, pricing pressure, or project delays.
If revenue softens while gross margin stays near 32%, the 23.4x trailing p/e stops looking reasonable very quickly.
∘
med
legal overhang can hit a small cap harder
The Schall Law Firm investigation may amount to little. Markets still dislike uncertainty, especially in a company with a $318M market cap and limited analyst coverage.
Even a modest legal distraction can weigh on sentiment, management time, and valuation for a stock this small.
with $103M of long-term debt, low predictability, and most revenue still tied to hardware, AVNW needs demand strength and margin discipline at the same time.
