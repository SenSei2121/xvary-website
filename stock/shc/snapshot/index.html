What they do
Sotera sterilizes medical products, tests them, and supplies the material that powers gamma sterilization.
More than 40 of the top 50 medical device companies and 9 of the top 10 global drugmakers use Sotera. terminal sterilization → the last sterilization step before shipping → so your product cannot leave without them. It is the only vertically integrated global gamma sterilization provider, which means one vendor can control the chain instead of three.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is ethylene oxide litigation and regulation.

∘
med
ethylene oxide litigation
the company faces lawsuits tied to ethylene oxide, the gas used in part of its sterilization process. this is the kind of risk that can sit quietly for quarters and then dominate the story again in one headline.
if settlements, fines, or operating restrictions escalate, a business with $2.3B of debt gets less flexible fast.
∘
med
premium multiple, thin earnings
the page shows SHC trading at 60.9x to 88.8x earnings against a 34.1x industry average. that is a premium multiple sitting on top of an EPS base around $0.21 and four years of flat earnings per share.
if the market stops paying up for durability, the re-rating risk comes before the operating risk does.
∘
med
large-holder selling overhang
Warburg Pincus sold 15M shares in a block trade. one sale does not define the business, but it can cap the stock while buyers absorb the supply.
with shares already down 24% this year, more selling pressure would keep sentiment weak even if operations stay stable.
a stock down 24% this year still carries a 60.9x–88.8x earnings multiple, backed by just $78M of net income and $2.3B of long-term debt. that is not a margin for error. that's a demand for clean execution.
