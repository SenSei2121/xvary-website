What could go wrong

the #1 risk is equity cancellation in the Chapter 11 liquidation. with $442M in debt and a disclosed $22M asset sale, the recovery math is pointed in one direction.

∘
med
equity sits last in line
Chapter 11 liquidation is not a normal restructuring story. Common stock only gets value after creditors are made whole.
with $442M in debt already disclosed, that hurdle is enormous relative to a ~$4M market cap.
∘
med
asset value looks far below debt value
The proposed $22M sale of the core business is the cleanest public datapoint for what someone will pay today.
$22M is roughly 5% of $442M. That gap is the whole bear case, and right now it is not really a bear case. It is arithmetic.
∘
med
negative gross margin leaves no operating cushion
A -53.7% gross margin means each additional dollar of revenue was not creating relief. It was adding more pressure.
that limits the chance that a short operating rebound materially improves the recovery stack for equity holders.
∘
med
delisting cuts visibility and liquidity
Nasdaq has moved to delist and deregister the stock, which now trades on OTC Pink under LAZRQ.
you lose exchange liquidity, institutional attention, and often the last thin layer of price discovery that small shareholders rely on.
a $4M market cap behind $442M in debt and a $22M asset sale leaves very little room for common equity to matter.
