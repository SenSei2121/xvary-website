What they do
Tandem sells insulin pumps and software that help people with diabetes dose insulin all day.
This business sits on your body, not just your phone. Once your pump, glucose monitor, and dosing software are working together, switching gets personal and annoying. Tandem already does about $1.0 billion in annual revenue, and its latest gross margin was 57.7%, which means the product still has pricing power even while expenses are eating the profit.

the number that mattered
The key number was EPS of -$0.01 versus a -$0.69 estimate because it proved the business can still surprise upward even while full-year losses remain ugly.
  • demand for tandem diabetes’ insulin pumps sagged as 2025 progressed. (the medical device maker is expected to release full-year 2025 results shortly after this report goes to press.) revenues advanced 10% during the first nine months, but the pace slowed to 2% in the third quarter.
    tandem shipped 88,000 pumps through the september period, compared with 87,000 in the like-2024 span, but only shipped 30,000 units in the third quarter of 2025, down from 31,000 a year earlier. at the three-quarter mark of 2025, the loss per share totaled $1.46, essentially even with the prior-year figure.
  • revenues likely increased 6% for 2025 as a whole, to $1 billion, but the bottom-line deficit probably widened to $2.15 per share.
  • margins have been under pressure due to elevated expenses.
  • the cost of goods sold expanded a modest 6% during the first nine months of 2025, but sg&a (selling, general, and administrative) expenses jumped 17%.
    on the plus side, the higher sg&a costs were caused by the buildout of sales infrastructure, which may yield better results down the road. research and development spending has been flattish, which could hinder product innovation in a competitive market.
  • finances have weakened over the last year.
