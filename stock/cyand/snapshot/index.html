What could go wrong

the top risk is single-facility dependence for both BioAstin and spirulina production.

∘
med
one site supports almost the entire revenue base
BioAstin generates $12.5M and spirulina generates $11.5M. Both run through the same production footprint.
A disruption does not hit one division. It hits most of the $24M business at once.
∘
med
debt sits above an equity stub
Long-term debt is $4M, or 62% of capital, while the market values the whole company at roughly $3M.
If operating results stay weak, shareholders keep owning the leftover claim after the lender gets paid.
∘
med
better margin without better sales is not enough
Q1 2026 gross profit rose to $1.8M, but sales still slipped 1.3% to $5.8M and the company remained loss-making.
If revenue does not stabilize, higher gross profit only slows the damage. It does not fix the thesis.
These risks touch almost all of the $24M revenue base and sit on top of $4M in debt. For a company valued at about $3M, that is survival math.
