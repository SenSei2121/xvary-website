What just happened
missed estimates
Kemper ended 2025 with December-quarter profit per share of $0.25, far below the $1.47 analyst estimate and down from $1.78 a year earlier.
The primary research source says the hit came from weak specialty property and casualty results, lower specialty personal auto volume, and reduced earned premiums from run-off business lines. Quiet part out loud: 80% of revenue sits in property and casualty, so that weakness drives the story.

the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $0.25 because it turned a cheap stock into a proof-required story. Until quarterly profits recover, the low multiple is not a bargain by itself.
  • kemper corporation ended a difficult 2025 with a sharp vs. prior year earnings decline during the december quarter.
    looking at it with added granularity, operating earnings per share dialed in at $0.25, which was markedly below the previous year’s $1.78 tally.
  • the primary reason for the sharp profit decline was a lackluster performance at the specialty p&c insurance unit, in particular the specialty personal auto segment.
    adjusted operating income here plummeted to around $2.6 million during the period, down from more than $101 million in the previous year. elevated bodily injury claim severity, especially in california, and higher claim frequency overall, increased losses.
  • aggregate revenues also declined during the period, which was another negative factor.
  • this was the result of lower specialty personal auto volume and a reduction in earned premiums for run-off business lines.
    kmpr stock, it should be noted, has been in a free-fall over the past year or so, resulting from the subpar earnings performance. we forecast a moderate recovery at the bottom line this year, while our freshly-inked 2027 estimates suggest sharper improvement. a key driver will be whether kemper can improve its combined ratios, especially in the specialty p/c auto insurance business. we are cautiously optimistic that it can, due to better pricing, more-efficient claims handling, and expense controls, which might well lead to underwriting profitability in that segment.
  • we look for the earnings rebound to continue over the 2029-2031 time frame.
    more-stringent underwriting standards, particularly in the specialty auto unit, along with rate increases underpin our long-term profit-growth assumptions.

What could go wrong

KMPR does not need a dramatic failure to disappoint you. It just needs claims, pricing, or estimates to drift the wrong way in a business already scoring 5/100 on predictability.

high
underwriting misses in auto and property/casualty
Insurance is a pricing business disguised as a financial stock. If Kemper prices risk badly, claim costs can wipe out the benefit of a 9.1x multiple fast.
This matters because the whole $4.8B revenue base depends on premiums staying ahead of claims. If that slips, the cheap stock gets cheaper for a reason.
