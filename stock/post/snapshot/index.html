the number that mattered
The 10% revenue growth mattered most because it showed Post can still grow the top line even in a transition year.
  • fiscal 2025 (ended september 30th) was another transition year for post holdings.
    the company continued to finetune its operations, making two acquisitions that add to the long line of deals made since the company was spun off from ralcorp holdings more than a decade ago. the most recent purchases included potato products of idaho last march and 8th avenue food & provisions in july.
  • the rationale behind the deals is to diversify the business and reduce its overall reliance on the more-mature and slowergrowth ready-to-eat cereal category.
  • during much of fiscal 2025, sales growth in the foodservice, weetabix, and refrigerated retail segments was offset by declines in the post consumer brands, the company’s largest unit.
  • pet food losses and cereal declines were the main culprits.
  • however, contributions from acquired businesses, the company’s focus on cost efficiency, and notable share buybacks allowed post to deliver a full fiscal year earnings-per-share advance of roughly 15%.
