the number that mattered

  • paramount skydance is making its debut in the institutional data.
  • the company was formed after the merger of paramount global and skydance media.
    for 2026, the newly formed paramount skydance is expected to see revenue of $30 billion, driven primarily by accelerated growth in its direct-to-consumer segment. paramount+ average revenue per user (arpu) should benefit from upcoming price increases and optimized subscriber mix, while the addition of live sports (ufc, zuffa boxing) and originals will strengthen engagement and retention.
  • tv media revenue may face moderate declines due to affiliate and advertising headwinds, partially offset by political spend and divestitures.
  • filmed entertainment will return to full-year profitability as the film slate is recalibrated.
    adjusted oibda (operating income before depreciation and amortization) is projected at $3.5 billion, supported by $3 billion in efficiency gains, targeted content, and technology investments. not long after the ink dried on the merger that created the new media giant, the company moved to make a bold acquisition.
  • in december 2025, netflix agreed to acquire the studio and streaming assets of warner bros.
    discovery, including warner bros. studios, hbo, and hbo max, in a cash-and-stock deal valued at about $82.7 billion ($72 billion equity), with closing expected in 18 months after wbd spins off its cable networks. however, soon after paramount skydance launched a hostile takeover bid for all of wbd at approximately $108 billion, arguing its all-cash offer was superior.
