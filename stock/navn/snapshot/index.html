What could go wrong

the #1 risk is missing the raised $685–$687M revenue guide before the profit story is credible.

∘
med
securities class action over sales and market risk disclosures
A lawsuit filed on March 6, 2026 alleges Navan failed to warn investors about sales and market risks. The lead-plaintiff deadline is April 7, 2026. That puts a legal headline over the stock while management is trying to tighten the operating story.
This does not need to threaten solvency to matter. It can still weigh on sentiment, management attention, and the stock's willingness to rerate.
∘
med
the raised guide is now the contract with investors
After about $170M of Q3 revenue, management lifted full-year guidance to $685–$687M. That is bullish. It also means the market now has one clean benchmark for whether the growth story is still working.
A miss or cut would hit the main rerating case directly because growth is still doing most of the valuation work.
∘
med
gross margin says software, net margin says the model is still unfinished
A 74% non-GAAP gross margin is strong. A -56.7% net margin is what is left after operating costs, stock compensation, and the rest of the income statement show up.
If costs do not grow slower than revenue from here, you are left owning a business with attractive top-line economics and weak reported earnings. The market usually notices the difference.
A forced hit to credibility would land on a company already asking investors to look past a -56.7% net margin. That makes execution risk and legal noise more expensive here than they would be for a finished software business.
