What they do
Duos sells inspection software, edge data services, and power consulting for railroads, remote markets, and AI infrastructure.
Duos uses machine vision, which is camera software that spots fast-moving objects, to inspect trains in real time. It also sells edge data services, which means local computers near users, so data moves faster. If you own it, you are betting that 79 employees can run rail, data, and power projects tied to 850 MW without tripping over each other.

What could go wrong

The top threat here is turning a $176M contract announcement into actual data center revenue without blowing up the share count.

∘
med
Hydra Host concentration
The new AI infrastructure narrative is tied to one customer and one $176M agreement. For a company that generated $28M in 2025 revenue, that is concentration on steroids.
If the contract is delayed, resized, or canceled, the main reason investors are paying ~8.3x sales disappears quickly.
∘
med
Dilution risk
Duos announced a proposed public offering on Feb 26, 2026. That tells you capital needs are not theoretical. They are current.
Even if revenue grows, your slice of the company can still shrink if new shares keep funding the buildout.
∘
med
Legacy business still has to carry the floor
The proven business generated $28M in 2025 with a 30.5% gross margin and negative trailing EPS. That is progress, not self-funding scale.
If the rail inspection business stalls before the new segment contributes, losses can stay sticky and financing pressure can rise again.
The risk stack is concentrated, quantifiable, and unusually binary: one contract, one funding question, one still-small core business.
