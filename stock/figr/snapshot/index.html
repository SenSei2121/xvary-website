What they do
Figure uses software and blockchain rails to originate loans, sell them to investors, and build new digital-asset plumbing.
Figure's edge is speed plus distribution. It pairs loan origination with Figure Connect, a marketplace that moves loans to buyers instead of parking them on its own balance sheet. You get a capital-light model (less funding burden, so more sales can turn into profit), and that helps explain a 23.2% operating margin on $507 million of revenue.

the number that mattered

  • this week, figure technology solutions is making its debut in the institutional data.
    the company is a relative newcomer to the burgeoning financial technology (fintech) sector, and held its initial public offering (ipo) on september 11, 2025. figure offers a suite of blockchain-based products and solutions that aim to promote efficiency and liquidity in financial markets. its technology-enabled loan origination system is paired with a distribution marketplace, dubbed figure connect, to facilitate access to capital markets. the company also leverages blockchain technology to develop an exchange for digital assets and credits, and provide interest-bearing stablecoin deposits.
  • there is the potential for rapid growth in the years ahead.
    the current infrastructure supporting capital markets is somewhat fragmented and depends on legacy systems. this can often lead to the slow processing of loan applications and transactions, creating cost inefficiencies and a bottleneck to the development of alternative marketplaces.
  • in a nutshell, figure and its ilk in the fintech space aim to migrate the recording of financial and real assets away from legacy systems that employ manual processing toward blockchain-based solutions.
    the technology that the company is developing holds great promise, but there are also a number of hurdles to clear. (figure is expected to release 2025 fourth-quarter financials in late february.) concentration risk is one issue worth noting, since the company derives essentially all of its revenues from its heloc (home equity line of credit) product. credit quality also bears watching, in the event that loans originated and purchased by figure underperform. too, as a financial institution, the regulatory environment, especially with regard to helocs, may impact the company’s operating results. in addition, although figure likely registered a profit for full-year 2025, it has a history of losses since being founded in 2018.
  • we advise waiting for a better entry point before making a commitment here.
    the stock has more than doubled in value since its ipo, limiting the price appreciation we foresee to late decade.
