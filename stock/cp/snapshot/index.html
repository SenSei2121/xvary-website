What they do
CPKC moves freight by rail across Canada, the U.S., and Mexico through one connected network.
CPKC owns about 20,300 miles of track across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico. It is the only transnational rail network in North America. If your grain or containers need all three countries, switching railroads is not a click. It is a logistics headache with steel attached.

What just happened
missed estimates
Latest quarter EPS came in at $0.76 versus a $1.10 estimate, a 30.91% miss.
Revenue in the December period rose nearly 8%, helped by record harvests in grain and currency effects. Then higher interest and tax expense showed up, and EPS fell 18%.

the number that mattered
The miss was 30.91%. For a stock with a 24.7x trailing P/E, that is the kind of gap that resets expectations fast.
  • fourth-quarter earnings at canadian pacific fell short of expectations.
  • revenues in the december period rose nearly 8%, as record harvests led to good growth in grain shipments, the company's largest category.
  • currency effects also boosted reported sales.
  • however, higher interest and tax expense put a damper on margins, and earnings per share fell 18% as a result.
  • the company is making good progress on efficiency measures, though, with average train speed accelerating by 4% and average train weight rising by 3%. we expect solid bottom-line improvement this year.
    grain harvests are expected to be strong again in 2026, with both canada and the u.s. feeding the strong appetite in the export market. the intermodal business is also expected to remain strong as new alliances are leading to higher volume. moreover, from the merger two years ago should accelerate now that the integration process has largely been completed. (we note that beginning this year, we are using adjusted earnings rather than gaap earnings.) our estimate is for earnings per share to reach $3.90 a share in 2026 and $4.40 a share in 2027, up from $3.37 in 2025. Management has stepped up its repurchase activity. the company had been restricted for a time from buying back its own stock due to the merger in 2023.

What could go wrong

Your biggest risk is the $1B merger-savings timeline slipping. At 24.7x earnings, this stock is priced like the integration work arrives on time and reaches shareholders cleanly.
