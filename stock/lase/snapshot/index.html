What could go wrong

the #1 risk is nasdaq compliance failure funded by more shareholder dilution. for a $32M company with a -105.39% net margin, financing risk is not separate from operating risk. it is the operating risk.

∘
med
Listing pressure and cheap warrants
A Nasdaq notice already hit the stock, and the company raised $1.5M through warrants at $1.08 per share. That price sits far below the current ~$2.37 quote.
If more capital comes in at similar terms, your ownership gets diluted before the turnaround has a chance to prove itself.
∘
med
Cash burn outruns backlog
The company reported a $4.7M net loss on $7.14M of trailing revenue and carries a -105.39% net margin. That means losses are still larger than the business is productive.
A $2.5M backlog helps only if it converts at acceptable margins. Otherwise you are financing operating losses, not scaling a model.
∘
med
Gross margin is not stable yet
Gross margin touched 53.5% in one quarter and sits at 35.22% in the latest period. That is a wide gap for a business this small.
When margins swing that much, one bad project mix can erase the benefit of a headline growth quarter.
With $7.14M of trailing revenue, a $4.7M loss, and $1.5M of recently issued warrants, the combined risk picture is simple: this business needs cleaner execution fast or the financing story keeps overwhelming the product story.
