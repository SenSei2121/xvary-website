the number that mattered
The 36.17% EPS surprise mattered most because it showed cost control and margin repair were better than expected in a thin-margin business.
  • dollar general delivered impressive fiscal third-quarter results (period ended october 31st).
  • earnings surged 44% from a year ago, to $1.28 a share.
    the performance was driven by improved traffic trends across all of the discount retailer’s core merchandise categories, with notable strength in consumables, seasonal, home products, and apparel. continued benefits from leadership’s store-expansion and turnaround initiatives were also supportive of comparisons.
  • we have raised our fiscal 2025 estimates.
  • dollar general has surpassed consensus expectations in three consecutive quarters, with leadership raising its full-year forecasts on each occasion.
    recent results have benefited from a significant consumer migration to valuebased shopping, likely motivated by persistently high inflation and increased economic strain. this shift in behavior has been broad-based across all income levels, with even higherend earners starting to prioritize price over brand. all in all, we are now targeting earnings to reach $6.50 a share this year, up from our previous call of $6.15.
  • the company is well positioned for continued bottom-line improvement in fiscal 2026.
    the combination of favorable macro trends and benefits from strategic growth initiatives augurs well for dollar general’s near-term earnings prospects. in regard to expansion, leadership expects to open roughly 450 new stores in the u.s. in fiscal 2026, and another 10 in mexico. these locations are mainly focused on rural areas and are expected to feature larger footprints with greater product assortments.

What could go wrong

Dollar General's problem is simple: a $40.6B sales base looks sturdy, but a 3.5% net margin means even small mistakes show up fast.
