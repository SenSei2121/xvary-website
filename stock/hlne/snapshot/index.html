What they do
Hamilton Lane helps big investors get into private equity, private credit, and secondaries, then takes fees for access, advice, and performance.

the number that mattered
The 34.8% EPS beat matters because it shows Hamilton Lane can turn asset growth into profit faster than the Street expected.
  • assets under management reached $145 billion, up 11% from 12 months earlier, while fee-earning aum rose to $76 billion, reflecting steady inflows and market appreciation.
  • business trends remain favorable across key strategies.
  • specialized funds, particularly the firm’s evergreen products, continue to drive growth, with record quarterly inflows and rising fee rates amid an asset-mix shift toward higher-margin offerings.
    management also announced a significant long-term partnership with guardian life, which is expected to contribute approximately $500 million annually over the next decade and further scale hamilton lane’s evergreen and insurance-focused platforms. the firm’s technology initiatives and data partnerships are also expanding engagement with institutional and wealth clients, supporting broader distribution and visibility into 2026 and beyond.
  • looking ahead, hamilton lane’s setup remains attractive.
    operating leverage is improving, fee-earning assets continue to grow, and expense discipline remains intact despite incremental investments tied to growth initiatives. the balance sheet is strong, dividend growth remains consistent, and unrealized paper gains that have not yet been sold continue to build.
  • the stock has increased nearly 25% in value since our late october report.
    despite the aforementioned stock price rise, these shares continue to hold attractive intermediate- and long-term capital return potential.
