What they do
Rigetti builds quantum computers and lets customers use them through its own cloud and larger cloud platforms.
Rigetti controls more of the stack than most peers. It designs the chip, builds the system, and sells access, which gives you more shots on goal from one lab.   • rigetti computing’s most significant development in recent months is a technical one.
    the core business focuses on building quantum computers that are both fast and accurate, two properties that are difficult to achieve simultaneously and are critical for the technology’s practical use.
  • in the december quarter, the company reached a milestone in accuracy: 99.9% fidelity on its two-qubit gate operations, the fundamental unit of quantum computation, on a prototype platform.
    this result affirms the company’s superconducting architecture as a viable path to large-scale quantum computing. the flagship 108-qubit cepheus-1-108q system, delayed after engineers identified interference problems between qubits at higher counts, was redesigned internally and is now aiming for deployment around the end of march.
  • according to management, only ibm has deployed a comparable system.
  • the order book reflects growing interest in on-premises hardware.
  • rigetti started 2026 with roughly $14.1 million in contracted system sales.
    this includes an $8.4 million order from india’s centre for development of advanced computing, or c-dac, for a 108-qubit system to be delivered in the second half of the year, and $5.7 million in orders for smaller 9qubit novera systems shipping in the first and second quarters. the c-dac deal is significant because it will be the first quantum computer purchased by the indian government, integrated into a national supercomputing environment.

What could go wrong

Rigetti's risk stack is unusually concentrated: one delayed flagship system, one tiny revenue base, and a market cap that already assumes eventual scale.
