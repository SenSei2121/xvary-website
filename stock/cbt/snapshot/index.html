What just happened
missed estimates
$849M in revenue and $1.37 in EPS were both lower than a year ago.
Revenue fell 11.0% vs. prior year, and EPS fell 18.0%. Gross margin was 24.9%, or about a quarter of sales left after direct costs.

Revenue was the tell. $849M was down 11.0%, so the demand weakness showed up before the full-year estimate did.
  • the company posted fourth-quarter earnings of $1.70 a share, on par with our estimate yet 6% below the year-ago figure, on a 10% decrease in the top line.
  • the reinforcement materials segment reported a 12.6% drop in sales, as volumes in the americas were impacted by lower production levels at its tire customers, given the increased levels of tire imports from asia into the region.
  • what’s more, performance chemicals’ sales fell 4.3%, due to a 5% decrease in volumes, largely the result of lower demand in europe.
    and leadership does not see signs of improvement in the current environment, particularly in reference to the impact of elevated asian tire imports into the americas.
  • hence, we have pared our earnings estimate for fiscal 2026 by $1.30 a share, to $6.50, on approximately a 4% drop in sales.
  • yet, investors seem relatively optimistic about the company’s long-term prospects.
