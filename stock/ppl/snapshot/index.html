What they do
PPL is a regulated utility that gets paid to deliver electricity to 3.7 million customers across four states.
Your power bill is the moat. PPL serves 3.7 million customers, and most of them are not comparison-shopping for new wires on Tuesday. Regulated utility model (state-approved returns on grid spending → the company earns on invested infrastructure → so what: the more approved projects it builds, the more predictable your earnings stream becomes) is boring by design.

the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $1.23 in quarterly EPS, because it shows how fast earnings can move when rate recovery and utility economics line up.
  • management reaffirmed its long-term target of 6%-8% annual earnings and dividends growth through at least 2028, with expectations skewed toward the upper end of that range.
    improved weather-normal demand and favorable regulatory mechanisms also contributed to stronger vs. prior year comparisons.
  • the company continues to advance its $20 billion infrastructure investment plan through 2028, supporting nearly 10% annual rate base growth.
    data center demand remains a key driver, particularly in pennsylvania, where the largeload pipeline expanded. regulatory progress in kentucky and pennsylvania has improved visibility around future returns, while capital allocation remains disciplined. management continues to emphasize customer protections and tariff structures designed to limit earnings volatility as large-load projects ramp.
  • shares are down about 3% in value since our last november report, despite improved earnings momentum.
  • the stock still remains up about 10% in value over the past 12 months.
    operating fundamentals remain solid, supported by regulated rate recovery, infrastructure spending, and expanding load demand.
  • execution around permitting, cost recovery, and conversion of the data center pipeline into contracted load will remain key swing factors.

What could go wrong

The #1 risk is regulators refusing full recovery on PPL's $20B–$23B capital build — this stock is priced like that spending will translate into earnings with minimal friction.
