What could go wrong

The #1 risk is clinical failure or delay for CP101 — when one asset carries most of the equity story, bad data does not hurt the thesis, it erases it.

∘
med
cash runway keeps shrinking
Finch had $16M in cash as of june 2024 and $100K of collaboration revenue. That means time is being funded, not growth.
If cash steps down and no financing or partnership shows up, the next capital raise starts from weakness.
∘
med
CP101 is the whole board
This is a single-asset setup in practice. There is no marketed product base to cushion a failed trial or regulatory setback.
A clinical miss would remove the main reason the market assigns value beyond cash and residual IP.

delisting makes financing harder
The planned Nasdaq delisting process, including a Form 25 filing expected around October 2024, puts liquidity at risk.
