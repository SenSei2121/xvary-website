What just happened
beat estimates
Last quarter, EQR delivered $0.98 EPS against a $0.61 estimate, a 60.7% beat.
The beat mattered because rent trends stayed firm. In the September interim, rental revenue rose 4.6%, same-store revenue rose 3.0%, same-store NOI rose 2.8%, and occupancy ended at 96.3%.

the number that mattered
The 60.7% EPS beat mattered because it showed operations held up better than the market expected.
  • equity residential reported solid results for the september interim.
  • rental revenue of $782.4 million grew 4.6% over the prior-year period tally of $748.3 million, driven by continued healthy operating fundamentals across most of its markets.
  • same-store portfolio revenues and net operating income (noi) increased 3.0% and 2.8%, respectively.
  • at quarter’s end, the portfolio consisted of 86,320 apartment units that included a 96.3% same-store physical occupancy rate, up 20 basis points from the previous year.
  • normalized funds from operations (nffo) increased 3.9% to $399.5 million, leading to $1.02 nffo per share.
