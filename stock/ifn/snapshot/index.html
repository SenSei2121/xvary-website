the number that mattered
$1.53M matters because it is the only hard quarterly operating figure in the source set, which leaves you leaning more on portfolio performance and payout policy.
  • the india fund struggled in 2025.
  • this occurred despite a solid performance for india-based equities in the fourth quarter.
  • for the 12-month period, the closed-end fund produced a total return of negative 7.1%, even when factoring in the impact of long-term distributions.
    there were a number of factors that weighed on the performance of india’s stock market and this country fund. the broad-based market was slowed by the rupee’s weakness against a basket of foreign currencies, disappointing corporate earnings growth earlier in the year, muted government spending and domestic consumption, and the stock market’s lack of exposure to the artificial intelligence (ai) trade. in addition, the increased u.s. tariffs on india’s exports to the united states after the countries failed to reach a new trade deal last year weighed some on the performance of india’s economy and stock market. not surprisingly, the india fund underperformed a number of its closed-end world equity fund peers in 2025.
