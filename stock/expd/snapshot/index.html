the number that mattered
The $1.49 EPS print mattered because it beat $1.05 by 41.9%, which is how freight stocks get forgiven for being boring.
  • thus, 2025 revenue and share earnings probably advanced to $11.03 billion and $5.90, respectively.
  • this year will primarily be defined by normalization.
    a correction in volume and pricing is expected, as importers work through the demand vacuum created by last year’s accelerated shipping activity.
  • the ocean freight business will likely face the most notable pressure.
    a fleet oversupply extending through 2026 has created excess capacity that is beginning to outweigh the impact of red sea diversions, placing downward pressure on rates.
  • hence, ocean volumes could experience a low-double-digit decline.
    by contrast, airfreight activity should remain relatively resilient, supported by high technology and pharmaceutical export volumes from north and south asia. in all, we expect 2026 revenue to pull back slightly to $11 billion, with share earnings growing modestly to $6.00.
  • nonetheless,..
    business prospects next year appear more favorable, as the industry moves past the normalization phase. expeditors is well positioned to benefit from the removal of the de minimis exemption, which now requires formal customs entries for a significant number of previously exempt commercial imports. the customs brokerage operations should see higher fee income, as shippers seek professional support to navigate an increasingly complex regulatory environment.
