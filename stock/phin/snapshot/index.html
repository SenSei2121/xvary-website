What they do
PHINIA makes fuel, electrical, and replacement parts that keep cars, trucks, and industrial engines running cleaner and longer.
PHINIA sells to most major original equipment manufacturers, which means your customers already built its parts into their vehicles and supply chains. That matters because changing suppliers in fuel and electrical systems is slow, expensive, and risky. The business still produced a 16.0% operating margin and 11.5% return on capital, according to.

the number that mattered
The key number was the 7.27% EPS beat, because it showed PHINIA finished 2025 better than expected even before the market started dreaming about $9.25 forward EPS.
  • phinia inc. closed out 2025 with stronger-than-anticipated results.
  • fourth-quarter revenues of $889 million were more than $40 million higher than our estimate and increased 7% compared to the previous-year tally.
    the advance was largely driven by favorable foreign exchange rates and customer recovery of tariffs.
  • the top line also got a boost from higher sales in asia and the americas, which more than offset lower volumes in europe.
  • the acquisition of swedish electromagnet invest ab (sem) last year also supported the gain.
  • share earnings of $1.18 were nearly a dime higher than our call and jumped 66% compared to the prior-year figure.
    the growth was supported by the higher revenues, favorable pricing, and cost-containment initiatives.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is combustion-engine demand slowing before PHIN finds a better mix.

high
