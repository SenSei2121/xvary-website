the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $137M of revenue, because a turnaround story with shrinking sales is still just a story.
  • stratasys turned in a mixed performance in 2025.
  • the company recorded net losses of $104 million, an improvement of 15% compared to $120 million in the prior year, while sales of $551 million declined 4% from a year ago.
  • the bottom-line performance was bolstered by record manufacturing-related revenue, which grew to 37.5% of total sales, up from just 25% in 2020.
    stratasys also ended the year with a fortified balance sheet, holding nearly $250 million in cash and short-term deposits against zero debt. operationally, the company is accelerating its transition into flightgrade and industrial production. management recently celebrated a major milestone in its airbus partnership, confirming that over 200,000 stratasys-certified parts are now in active service across the airbus fleet. expansion in the automotive sector has also been a key theme, with subaru of america implementing the f770 printer to reduce tooling development time by over half. additionally, to streamline customer workflows, stratasys also launched a new post processing partnership program in january, integrating automated finishing solutions directly into its additive manufacturing ecosystem.
  • for 2026, stratasys expects a return to top-line growth.
  • full-year revenue is projected to range between $565 million and $575 million, a roughly 3% increase at the midpoint from 2025 levels.
    management anticipates sequential improvement throughout the year, with the second half expected to be stronger, as new hardware and software launches targeting aerospace and automotive gain traction. however, the company is bracing for approximately $17 million in combined adverse impacts from foreign exchange and tariffs. despite these headwinds, stratasys remains focused on its long-term goal of becoming the first choice for polymer additive manufacturing, leveraging its large installed base and growing recurring consumables revenue to drive sustained bottom-line growth.
