beat estimates

Annual revenue was $1.8B, up 1.3% vs. prior year, so the quarter was unusually distorted versus the prior-year comparison. Quiet part loud: one hot quarter does not erase a weak full-year profit base.

the number that mattered
The number that mattered was $1.4B in quarterly revenue because it shows the asset base can still generate cash even while forward earnings estimates fall.
  • vornado has declared a full-year 2025 dividend of $0.74 per share. (the reit is scheduled to release december-period financials in early february.) the distribution will be paid on december 29th to shareholders of record on december 18th, marking the only dividend for the year soon ending.
    recall that in early 2023, management reduced the quarterly payout from $0.53 per share to $0.375, due to the uncertain domestic economy and weakened demand for office space. in april of 2023, vornado revised its dividend policy whereby the distribution would be determined at the conclusion of the year when its taxable income was calculated.
  • meanwhile, operating metrics in the company’s commercial portfolio have improved.
  • in the recent third quarter, occupancy in vornado’s commercial segments was 87.5% in new york (up from 86.7% in the prior-year period), 80.7% in themart (compared with 79.7%), and 96.3% in 555 california (versus 94.5% a year ago).
  • on the residential side of the business, occupancy dropped from 96.5% at the same point in 2024 to 93.7%.
  • moreover, occupancy here declined throughout 2025, a trend that bears watching.
