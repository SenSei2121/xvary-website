What they do
National Bankshares is a community bank that takes local deposits, makes loans, and sells some insurance and investment products.
This is old-school community banking with actual roots. National Bank of Blacksburg runs 28 locations, 25 ATMs, and one loan office across southwest and central Virginia, with $1.6 billion of deposits at September 30, 2025. Scale loses to proximity here. When your business account, mortgage, and local lender all sit in one bank, leaving is annoying.

What could go wrong

The top risk is margin compression at a Southwest Virginia community bank under new leadership.

∘
med
Interest rate sensitivity
Net interest income is $26.2M out of the $30.8M revenue mix shown here. If deposit costs rise faster than loan yields, the spread narrows and most of the earnings story narrows with it.
This risk touches roughly 85% of the revenue mix shown on the page.
∘
med
CEO transition
F. Brad Denardo retired on June 30, 2025. New CEO Lara E. Ramsey does not get a long grace period at a bank this size — strategy changes show up fast in deposit pricing, loan growth, and expense control.
Execution risk is elevated because management change and earnings volatility are happening at the same time.
~
low
Geographic concentration
This is a Southwest Virginia bank. That local focus can help relationships, but it also means one regional slowdown can hit loans, deposits, and credit quality at once.
You do not have broad geographic diversification to offset a local downturn.
∘
med
Dividend relying on lower earnings
A 4.2% yield looks good. It looks less comfortable when last year's profit was only $7.6M after a 51% decline.
If earnings do not stabilize, the yield stops feeling like a cushion and starts feeling like a question.
The income case depends on a bank with $7.6M of last-year profit keeping its spread intact while a new CEO takes over. That is specific risk, not generic bank risk.
