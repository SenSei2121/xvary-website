What could go wrong

The top risk here is Middle East project timing and shipping disruption. Perma-Pipe is small enough that one delayed contract can distort a quarter.

Shipping lanes and cross-border execution
Management has already flagged shipping lane risk tied to its Middle East project pipeline. That matters more when international project work is a growth driver, not a side note.
The immediate issue is timing. A delayed shipment can push revenue, margin absorption, and investor confidence from one quarter into the next.

med
Project concentration
A $43M contract is material for a company with a $228M market cap and $200.78M in trailing revenue. That is good when execution is clean. It is painful when one job carries too much of the narrative.
If large projects slip or margins disappoint, the Street's 57.44% EPS growth forecast stops looking ambitious and starts looking wrong.
∘
med
Leadership transition
The CEO retired in June 2025, and the business is now asking investors to underwrite rapid growth during a handoff. That is not fatal. It is not irrelevant either.
When a company has a C++ balance sheet and 20/100 predictability, even a clean leadership change still adds another moving part.
The risk stack is simple: lumpy contracts, cross-border execution, and a management transition are sitting underneath a growth story the market already expects to be dramatic.
