What could go wrong

the #1 risk is municipal contract slippage in ShotSpotter and CrimeTracer. when you run a roughly $102M revenue business, a few delayed or lost public-sector contracts show up everywhere — growth, margins, and investor patience.

∘
med
ShotSpotter attrition is real, not theoretical
management expects about $3M of annual recurring revenue attrition in 2026 from customer budget pressure. against a roughly $102M revenue base, that is material.
impact: that attrition alone equals roughly 3% of annual revenue before you even ask the rest of the business to grow.
∘
med
the guidance reset may not be the last one
2026 revenue guidance fell to $109M–$111M from $114M–$116M because of CrimeTracer and Puerto Rico delays. that is a noticeable cut for a company this size.
impact: if timing slips again, the valuation discount can stay in place even with a low 0.8x sales multiple.
∘
med
margin compression is attacking the entire bull case
gross margin dropped to 54% from 58%, while full-year revenue grew just 2% and adjusted EBITDA fell to $1.3M from $1.7M. the business did not show operating leverage.
impact: a forced lower-margin profile would keep net margin stuck near the current -10.44% zone.
∘
med
leadership turnover adds one more variable
Nasim Golzadeh's resignation, effective march 31, 2026, creates uncertainty inside the TechnoLogic and Investigative Solutions units at the same time execution already looks uneven.
impact: this does not change the math by itself, but it raises the odds of more delays in a business that cannot afford many.
a $3M attrition hit, lower 2026 guidance, and a four-point gross-margin decline all point to the same conclusion: SSTI is not one fix away from clean profitability.
