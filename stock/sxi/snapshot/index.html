the number that mattered
41.6% gross margin mattered most because margin expansion is the cleanest evidence that this is more than an acquisition-fueled revenue story.
  • standex international began fiscal 2026 on strong footing. (year ends on june 30th.) sales for the september period came in at the high end of expectations, at $217.4 million, achieving 28% vs. prior year growth.
  • momentum in electrical grid modernization and renewable energy demand boosted electronics.
    meanwhile, new product launches (four in the quarter), acquisition-related benefits, and organic growth in core areas added tailwinds.
  • what’s more, operational efficiencies and site consolidations supported margin expansion.
  • overall, standex built on a record fiscal 2025, where the company achieved high margins and significant acquisition-driven growth.
  • the company is expected to do very well in the near term.
    we anticipate electronics demand to remain robust, supported by ongoing electrical grid investments and strong backlog. in fact, the book-to-bill ratio continued to hover above 1.0x, suggesting that business is taking in orders faster than it can ship them. meanwhile, standex is committed to launch 15 new products this fiscal year, particularly in the electric vehicle and medical sectors, which could build on recent momentum.
