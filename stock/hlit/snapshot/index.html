the number that mattered
Revenue growth of 190% mattered most because it shows how dramatic the business mix shift has become, even if the annual base is still down 26.2%.
  • harmonic’s broadband segment is doing the heavy lifting.
  • december-quarter broadband revenue of $98.2 million exceeded the top of its $85 million-$95 million guidance range, with the upside driven by accelerating service deployments and a 9% sequential increase, as customers began connecting subscribers on the cos platform.
    the cos platform is harmonic’s cloud-native virtualized cable modem termination system, and software license revenue is recognized when operators actually activate subscribers rather than when hardware ships, meaning the december quarter’s outperformance reflected a genuine ramp in live network deployments rather than pull-forward shipments.
  • recurring SaaS and services revenue accounted for 16% of broadband revenue in the quarter, with management noting the software component is growing, as more of the 146 commercial cos deployments move from integration into active subscriber expansion.
  • the latest bookings data is encouraging.
  • the company logged a record $346.9 million in broadband orders during the december quarter, a 3.5 book-to-bill ratio that expanded total backlog and deferred revenue by 73% vs. prior year to $573.8 million.
    a book-to-bill ratio measures new orders against fulfilled revenue; a 3.5 reading means the company booked $3.50 in future commitments for every dollar it recognized.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is cableos deployment delays at major operators.

backlog fails to convert on schedule
Revenue is recognized when subscribers actually go live, not when boxes ship or contracts get signed. That makes timing risk very real even with strong orders.
With $573.8M of backlog and deferred revenue against $361M of annual revenue, slippage hits the whole story fast.
∘
med
orders normalize after one huge quarter
December-quarter broadband orders were a record $346.9M and book-to-bill was 3.5. Great numbers. Also hard numbers to lap.
If bookings cool meaningfully from here, the $440M–$480M 2026 revenue range gets less comfortable.
∘
med
broadband strength does not fully offset the rest of the business
Total revenue still fell 46.9% last year. Broadband is doing the heavy lifting, but it still has to outrun weakness elsewhere.
