What they do
IDT sells calling, money transfer, and retail software services to everyday customers and small stores.
IDT wins by sitting inside repeat, low-glamour habits. If your store runs on NRS or your family sends cash through BOSS Money, switching is annoying, retraining is real, and daily workflow breaks. That helps the business earn a 31.5% return on capital, which is elite for a company with just a 7.5% operating margin.

the number that mattered
37.2% gross margin matters most because it shows IDT is shifting toward better businesses, even while legacy communications still dominates revenue.
  • IDT Corporation certainly started off fiscal 2026 (year ends july 31st) with a bang.
  • indeed, the company posted october-quarter adjusted earnings of $0.89 a share, well above our estimate and a hefty 32% improvement on the year-ago result, on a more modest 4% uptick in sales.
  • it came as no great surprise that traditional communications revenues (68.0% of first-quarter revenues versus 71.2% this time last year) continue to contract, while the company's higher-margined businesses (nrs, net2phone, and fintech) are growing at double-digit rates.
    additionally, the gross profit margin widened by 180 basis points during the recently completed quarter, thanks to management's emphasis on reducing overhead and streamlining operations companywide. all told, we have upped our fiscal 2026 earnings estimate by a dime, to $3.45 a share, and we have initiated a $3.65 call for the following fiscal year.
  • nevertheless, the investment community does not seem enthused with the company's prospects.
  • to wit, despite the better-than-expected fiscal first-quarter performance, idt stock is up a modest 1% since our early december review, versus a 3% uptick in the s&p 500 index over the same time frame.
