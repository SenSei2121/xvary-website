What could go wrong

The top disclosed overhang here is the Barbara J. Comly shareholder lawsuit, but the operating risk is just as clear: this is a small revenue base with little room for margin slippage.

∘
med
Barbara J. Comly lawsuit
A shareholder lawsuit filed in June 2024 alleges financial reporting issues. Legal overhang is bad enough on a megacap. On a $204M company, it can dominate the narrative.
If confidence in prior disclosures erodes, the problem is not just legal cost. It can hit customer trust and financing flexibility at the same time.
∘
med
gross margin pressure
Gross margin was 51.3%, and management has already said 2026 will be challenging during a product transition. That is the quiet part loud.
A five-point margin drop would erase about $2.8M in annual gross profit based on a $55.2M revenue base. For a near-break-even company, that matters a lot.
∘
med
niche market ceiling
Everspin says it holds 30% of a $912M MRAM market. The good news is leadership. The bad news is the market is still under $1B.
If the company cannot expand beyond its core niche, growth stays tethered to a market that analysts already model at just 7.4% annual growth.
∘
med
customer and product concentration
91.7% of revenue comes from MRAM product sales, and the moat description itself points to reliance on a few key industrial and automotive customers.
When one product family drives almost the entire company, a qualification delay or customer pause hits nearly the whole revenue base, not a side business.
Almost the entire $55.2M revenue base depends on MRAM adoption holding up while gross margin stays near 51.3%. That is a narrow path.
