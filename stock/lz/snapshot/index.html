What they do
LegalZoom sells online legal help to people and small businesses, from forming an LLC to handling trademarks and estate plans.
Legal problems are annoying, repetitive, and expensive. LegalZoom makes them feel like software instead of a law office. It serves all 50 states and more than 3,000 counties, which means you get a national front door with local reach. Return on capital was 32.1% in 2024. Return on capital → profit from each dollar invested → this business converts a small asset base into solid economics.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is AI turning legal documents into a cheaper commodity. That goes straight at the part of the model supporting 71% gross margins.

∘
med
AI document generation gets good enough
If customers can draft formation docs, contracts, and filings with cheaper AI tools, LegalZoom's core convenience advantage narrows fast.
A hit to pricing would matter immediately because 71% gross margin is doing most of the business-model heavy lifting.
∘
med
2026 growth lands closer to 3.2% than 8%
Last year's full-business growth was 3.2%. Management is guiding for 8% next. If that reacceleration fails, the stock still has a 77x trailing p/e attached to a low-growth profile.
The valuation would have less room to hide because premium multiples need visible acceleration, not just a good quarter.
∘
med
High gross margin never turns into strong net margin
A 71% gross margin business posting only a 2.0% net margin is telling you customer acquisition, support, and operating expense are still eating most of the benefit.
If scale does not improve profitability, investors stop treating this like a platform and start treating it like a marketing-heavy service business.
∘
med
Regulatory or legal scrutiny adds friction
The company's own filings cite investigations and lawsuits as material risks. For a business built around legal workflows, trust and compliance are not side issues.
Even without a disclosed dollar amount here, extra legal cost or operating limits would pressure already-thin bottom-line margins.
A slowdown from 13% Q4 growth to 8% guided 2026 growth would already test sentiment. If that comes with pressure on the 71% gross margin or the 2.0% net margin stays stuck, the premium multiple becomes the risk.
