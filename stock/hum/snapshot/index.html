the number that mattered
The key number was -$3.96 EPS. Sales grew, but profit still missed, which says the cost problem is still in the room.
  • humana reported fourth-quarter results that met expectations.
  • december-period revenues of $32.5 billion represented an 11.6% vs. prior year increase, with the top line again driven by higher per-member medicare and state-based contract premiums.
    the adjusted loss per share of $3.96 in the seasonallyweak december interim nonetheless allowed for a full-year share-net tally of $17.14, slightly above projection. full-year results were underpinned by solid performance across the insurance and centerwell segments.
  • leadership provided a tepid outlook for 2026.
  • adjusted earnings per share of ‘‘at least $9.00’’ are projected, representing a steep annual decline.
    humana is facing a substantial star ratings headwind that figures to impact the top line by as much as $3.5 billion, owing to a drop in cms (centers for medicare & medicaid services) star ratings for the company’s medicare advantage (ma) plans. cms utilizes a five-star rating system to evaluate humana’s plans based on member experience, call center accuracy, safety, and health services, which, in turn, influences the degree of bonus payments made to the insurer.
  • the percentage of humana’s ma members who were enrolled in 4+ star plans plunged from 94% in 2024 to 25% in 2025, thereby impacting bonus eligibility, compressing margins, and impacting earnings visibility.

What could go wrong

Humana's biggest problem is Medicare Advantage economics breaking at the same time as star ratings slip. This is a reimbursement and cost-control story, not a branding story.

!
high
medical cost inflation
Care costs are rising faster than pricing and reimbursement can offset. Management already tied this to a much weaker 2026 earnings outlook.
The proof is in the numbers: full-year EPS went from $17.14 in 2025 to guidance of at least $9.00 for 2026.
!
high
star ratings pressure
CMS star ratings drive bonus payments in Medicare Advantage. When quality scores drop, the economics get worse fast.
Humana said this pressure could hit revenue by as much as $3.5B, while 4+ star membership fell from 94% to 25%.
