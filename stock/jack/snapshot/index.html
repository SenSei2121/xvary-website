what it is
Jack in the Box runs and franchises fast-food burger restaurants, with 2,136 locations and most of them operated by franchisees.
how it gets paid
Fiscal 2025 revenue was about $1.47B (down ~6.7% vs. prior year). The largest line item was company restaurant sales (~$0.63B, ~43% of revenue), with franchise rental (~$0.37B), royalties and other (~$0.23B), and franchise advertising contributions (~$0.24B) making up the rest.
why growth slowed
Revenue fell about 6.7% last fiscal year. Latest reported quarter revenue was $326.2 million, down 6.6% vs. prior year. Jack in the Box system same-store sales were down 7.4% in that quarter and down 4.2% for fiscal 2025.
what just happened
Q4 fiscal 2025 adjusted EPS was $0.30, below the Zacks consensus of about $0.46 (a miss). Revenue of $326.2 million was slightly ahead of the consensus sales estimate.

What they do
Jack in the Box runs and franchises fast-food burger restaurants, with 2,136 locations and most of them operated by franchisees.
This is a franchise machine, not just a burger chain. Jack in the Box has 1,986 franchised stores out of 2,136 total units, so other people put up much of the restaurant capital while the company collects fees. You also get density where it matters: California and Texas alone hold 1,490 stores, or 70% of the system, which keeps the brand visible and the supply chain efficient.

What just happened
missed EPS
Q4 fiscal 2025 adjusted EPS was $0.30, below the Zacks consensus of about $0.46. Revenue of $326.2M was slightly ahead of the consensus sales estimate.
Revenue was down 6.6% vs. prior year (from $349.3M). Jack in the Box system same-store sales fell 7.4% in the quarter (down 4.2% for the full fiscal year). Translation: earnings missed, and traffic was still weak.

the number that mattered
The key number was the 7.4% same-store sales drop in the quarter, because one quarter of EPS versus estimates matters less than customers visiting less often.
  • jack in the box has completed the sale of the del taco chain.
    in december, yadav enterprises paid $119 million to acquire the roughly 575-unit quickservice concept. jack had purchased del taco less than four years ago for $585 million, including debt, but same-store sales there have been declining for nearly the past two years. selling the business has freed up cash for debt reduction and advanced the company’s shift toward a simpler, asset-light business model.
  • the company still has work to do at the flagship chain.
    jack in the box too has struggled through an extended period of soft sales, with trends getting steadily weaker over the course of fiscal 2025.
  • system-wide comps for the final quarter, which ended on september 28th, fell 7.4%.
    same-store sales probably remained in negative territory through the december period, but ought to stabilize, with a flat comparison likely for the full fiscal year. the backdrop for quickservice restaurants may well remain challenging, though recent initiatives to implement additional training and supervision, a shift toward more-value oriented marketing strategies, and promotional events surrounding the chain’s 75th anniversary ought to provide a lift.
  • we are cutting our earnings estimates for fiscal 2026. (note that our presentation excludes del taco as of the start of the fiscal year.) adjusted ebitda is expected to finish between $225 million and $240 million, versus $271 million for the combined company in 2025.
  • interest costs should decline a bit, and savings from the streamlining of general and administrative costs ought to build as the year progresses.
