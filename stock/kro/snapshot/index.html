What they do
Kronos makes titanium dioxide, the white pigment that makes paint, plastic, paper, and cosmetics look bright and opaque.
This is a scale commodity business, which means size and customer reach matter more than storytelling. Kronos sells to 3,000 customers in 100 countries, and 59% of sales go into coatings, so you are plugged into everyday products people keep buying even in slow years. The catch is simple: 99% of sales still come from TiO2, so the moat is distribution, not product variety.

  • kronos worldwide’s 2025 fourth-quarter results were likely down from the year-ago numbers. (the company is due to report results on march 10th.) demand for kronos’ only product, titanium dioxide (tio2) pigment (99% of sales), has been slipping.
  • this chemical is used in a wide variety of consumer and commercial products as a whitening agent.
    customers are reverting to just-in-time inventory methods as economic growth uncertainties become pervasive.
  • the lack of demand probably resulted in kronos having to reduce its selling prices.
    in addition, rising raw material costs due to higher tariffs, and less efficient supply chains, likely caused the bottom line to sink to a deficit of around $0.25.
  • kronos is cutting back production of tio2 in order to better meet an anticipated lower demand environment in 2026.
  • to wit, it’s consolidating its five european plants to four, and implementing more stringent cost cuts at its four north american facilities.
