What they do
Heartland Express moves time-sensitive freight on short and medium truck routes, mostly east of the Rockies.
This business wins when shippers need freight moved fast and without drama. Heartland's average haul is under 400 miles, which keeps trucks turning faster and customers sticky when their loads are time-sensitive. The balance sheet still matters too: long-term debt is $154 million, or 16% of capital, which gives you more room than a heavily levered trucker when rates stay ugly.

The number that mattered was the -242.86% earnings miss, because it tells you the recovery is taking longer than analysts expected.
  • revenues likely fell for the third consecutive year in 2025, and profits again proved elusive.
    the trucking industry is mired in an unusually deep and long recession that shows few signs of easing.
  • the trouble stems back to the covid pandemic.
  • at that time, people were stuck at home and took to ordering everything online.
  • this led to a sharp surge in shipping demand, which in turn prompted truckers to add significant capacity.
    with workers returning to their offices, shipping demand has fallen back to more normal levels, but capacity remains out of balance.
  • a leaner more efficient business should produce healthy margins once demand returns.
    heartland operates four trucking companies, two of them were purchased in 2022 just as the market was peaking.
