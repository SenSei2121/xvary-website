the number that mattered
Communications revenue of $351.9 million matters most because that one segment grew 51% and is carrying the current story.
  • specifically, revenue for communications surged 51% vs. prior year, to $351.9 million, driven almost exclusively by hyperscale data center demand.
  • meanwhile, infrastructure solutions benefited from the same secular tailwind, and revenue jumped 30% over the same span, to $140.2 million.
  • top-line growth for commercial & industrial was more modest, though, at a 7% rate, to $94.8 million, also boosted by tailwinds associated with data center proliferation.
  • however, a soft housing market continued to pressure volumes for its residential business.
  • that segment saw revenues dip 11%, to $284.1 million.
