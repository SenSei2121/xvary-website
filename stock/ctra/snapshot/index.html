What just happened
missed estimates
Coterra posted Q4 adjusted EPS of $0.39, missing the $0.51 consensus by 23.53% even as revenue beat.
Quarterly revenue reached $1.8 billion in Q4 2025, according to the company, but profit did not keep up with the sales line. Last reported annual revenue was $7.3 billion, up 33.6% vs. prior year, which is the classic energy setup: big top-line swing, thinner visibility on the bottom line.

the number that mattered
The number that mattered was the 23.53% EPS miss, because commodity stocks get punished faster for earnings misses than they get rewarded for revenue beats.
  • coterra energy has agreed to an allstock buyout offer from devon energy.
    under the terms of the pending deal, coterra investors will receive 0.70 of a devon share for each ctra share, implying a combined enterprise value of $58 billion. both companies hold significant positions in the permian basin, with devon owning around 400,000 acres and coterra another 345,000 acres. management expects the transaction (scheduled to close in the 2026 second quarter) to generate $1.0 billion in.
  • efficiency targets have become even more important amid volatile energy markets.
  • investor approval would be needed for any potential tie-up.
  • kimmeridge energy management has expressed support for the transaction.
    kimmeridge was previously seeking to nominate scott sheffield (the former ceo of pioneer natural resources) to the company’s board, with the goal to force coterra to sell its natural gas assets and focus on crude production in the permian.
  • this comes at a time management has sharpened its strategy.
    coterra intends to enhance operational flexibility through the deepening reliance of a multi-basin production framework. moreover, it continues to operate under the assumption that strong liquified natural gas (lng) demand and growing electricity usage will remain key catalysts in the short and long terms.
