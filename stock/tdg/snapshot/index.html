What they do
TransDigm makes niche aircraft parts that airlines and militaries keep buying because planes cannot fly without them.

the number that mattered
The 27.0% EPS surprise matters most because it shows this company can still outrun expectations even with acquisition-related pressure in the background.
  • transdigm’s share price fell markedly after releasing mixed fiscal 2026 first-quarter results. (year ends september 30th.) the top-line performance was sound, with revenues of $2.29 billion increasing 13.9% vs. prior year.
  • excluding the impact of acquisitions, we estimate a still solid top-line growth rate of 9.5% for the december period.
  • quarterly earnings on the other hand grew by just 5.1%, as margins were pressured some by the $100 million servotronics acquisition completed in july and the $765 million simmonds precision products purchase that closed in october.
    another spree of recent acquisition agreements, plus the company’s updated outlook for fiscal 2026, suggest a continuation of narrower profit margins over the intermediate term.
  • transdigm’s track record on executing its growth-via-acquisition strategy gives us confidence that its recent brisk m&a activity will pay off.
    servotronics, though small, expanded transdigm’s manufacturing portfolio into the servo controls market. these systems are used to precisely manage the position, velocity, or torque of a motor, allowing for highly accurate movement in robotics and industrial automation. the simmonds purchase from rtx corp. strengthened the portfolio by adding specialized fuel & proximity sensing and structural health monitoring solutions for the aerospace and defense (a&d) end markets.
  • in keeping with transdigm’s blueprint for success, simmonds products are highly engineered and nearly all proprietary, making it the sole manufacturer of critical aftermarket parts on 40% of its $350 million in annual revenue.
    at the start of the new calendar year, transdigm announced agreements with private equity firms to acquire stellant systems for $960 million and jets parts engineering (jpe) plus victor sierra aviation (vsa) for $2.2 billion.
