the number that mattered
The real number was the 3.4% annual revenue decline, because a turnaround without sales stability is just cost-cutting in a lab coat.
  • avantor should post vs. prior year top- and bottom-line declines in 2025.
  • we expect the life sciences and advanced technology company to post a net loss around $0.65 per share, down against the profit of $1.04 per share in the prior-year, with sales contracting 4%, to $6.55 billion.
    both laboratory solutions and bioscience production segments have faced headwinds throughout 2025 due to customer funding and competitive pressures. this culminated in the third-quarter with avantor reporting a $785 million non-cash goodwill impairment charge. the impairment was the result of soft demand in the laboratory solutions segment, particularly in education and government end markets as well as inefficiencies in bioprocessing.
  • for 2026, the company has launched a revival plan.
    this plan focuses on simplifying the company’s complex operating model and evolving its go-to-market strategy in order to regain lost market share.
  • avantor is also pruning its portfolio to focus on higher-growth, higher-margin areas and rationalizing lower-growth businesses.
    emphasis is being placed on the bioscience production segment which is showing stronger margins and positive organic growth compared to the more challenged laboratory solutions segment. management is also looking toward the phase-in of roughly $100 million in new business wins from top-tier global pharmaceutical customers expected this year. these contracts, combined with an expected stabilization in biotech funding and a reduction in inventory destocking, are intended to provide a much-needed tailwind for volume growth as the company moves past its 2025 trough.
  • longer term, avantor’s trajectory will be heavily influenced by its cost transformation.
    set for completion by the end of 2027, this includes amending credit agreements, deleveraging, and removing $400 million in overhead expenses annually.

What could go wrong

Avantor's main risk is not some vague "macro" bucket. It is laboratory solutions demand staying weak while the turnaround takes longer than promised.

∘
med
lab demand stays soft
Education and government end markets were weak enough to help trigger a $785M impairment. If that demand does not recover, revenue pressure does not magically fix itself.
impact: the company already expects 2025 sales of $6.55B, down 4%. Another year of weak volumes would make the turnaround feel less like a delay and more like the new baseline.
∘
med
the $400M cost plan slips
Management is promising a lot by the end of 2027: a simpler operating model, portfolio pruning, and $400M of annual overhead removed. Turnarounds usually look easy on slide 12.
impact: if the savings are late or smaller than promised, the hoped-for margin recovery gets pushed out while you are still carrying a business expected to lose $0.65 per share in 2025.
∘
med
bioscience production does not offset the weakness
The company is leaning on higher-margin bioscience production and roughly $100M of new pharma wins to help stabilize growth. That is a real lever, but it is also doing a lot of narrative heavy lifting.
impact: if those wins phase in slowly, or positive organic growth fades, the stock loses the cleanest argument for why 2025 is a trough instead of a trend.
If earnings do not recover in 2026, the cheap multiple stops looking cheap and starts looking accurate.
