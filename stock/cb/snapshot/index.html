the number that mattered
The $7.52 result beat the $6.34 estimate by 18.61%, which says Chubb is still managing losses and pricing well.
  • chubb limited closed out 2025 with solid results.
    after starting the year off weakly as a result of policy exposure to massive wildfires in los angeles county, this leading property and casualty (p/c) insurer posted successively higher operating earnings over the final three quarters of 2025.
  • certainly, lower catastrophe losses in the back half of the year were a factor. (in 2025, insurers benefited from a surprisingly mild atlantic hurricane season, with no major storms making landfall in the continental u.s. versus five in the prior year.) acquisitions in recent years, which provided significant exposure to the asia-pacific region, are paying off in terms of growth.
    the diversification gained from adding accident and health lines, as well as boosting life insurance, has also been a plus.
  • finally, despite a slightly lower rate of return in 2025 versus 2024, revenue from invested income grew 9% to $6.47 billion on an expanding portfolio (up 11% in size in 2025 and 9% in 2024).
  • business momentum has picked up recently, and, if trends endure, the company should see roughly 8% to 11% earnings-per-share growth this year and next. (that assumes reasonable catastrophe losses.) the quarterly growth rate for net premiums written (npw), from a year ago and excluding the impact of shifts in currency, was up in the fourth period of 2025 relative to the third period.
    the npw top-line metric is important because over the span of a number of months, npw is recognized as earned revenue or ‘‘net premiums earned.’’ hence, npw is one key driver of earnings growth, comparable to sales growth for an industrial company.
  • consolidated npw grew 8.3% in constant dollars for the december quarter versus 6.6% in the september quarter.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is catastrophe losses snapping back after a mild hurricane season.

!
high
major hurricane or wildfire season
Insurance earnings are smooth until nature decides otherwise. Chubb just posted $2.98B of quarterly core operating income because catastrophe losses were lower. That can reverse fast.
A bad season can erase something close to a $3B quarter.
∘
med
insurance pricing softening
Premium growth was 8.9% in the latest quarter, and constant-dollar growth improved to 8.3% from 6.6% in Q3. If the hard market softens, that tailwind weakens.
Slower pricing would pressure margins and the current 8%–11% EPS growth view.
∘
med
shipping and geopolitical exposure
Chubb's role in a $20B Hormuz shipping backstop ties part of the business to a trade corridor nobody wants in the headlines. Escalation could raise claims and reinsurance costs at the same time.
That would hit both underwriting results and the cost of covering future risk.
~
low
the diversification story underdelivering
Accident and health is only $5.9B, or 10% of revenue. It helps, but it is not large enough to fully offset a bad property year.
Diversification can soften a blow without preventing it.
A mild storm year makes Chubb look cheap. A rough one reminds you why insurers rarely get premium multiples.
