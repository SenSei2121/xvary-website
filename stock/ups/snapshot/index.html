the reconciliation
Street headlines often use adjusted Q4 EPS ($2.38) while GAAP prints lower ($2.10)—both are in the release; know which one you are tracking.
  • revenue per piece jumped 8.3%, marking the strongest fourth-quarter growth in four years and more than offsetting a 10.8% drop in average daily volume, driven largely by the deliberate exit from low-margin amazon business.
  • non-GAAP adjusted diluted EPS was $2.38 for Q4 2025 vs. GAAP $2.10—compare either figure to the consensus feed you trust.
  • small- and medium-sized business penetration reached a record 31.2% of u.s. volume, while the business-to-business mix climbed to 37.5%, the highest holiday-quarter reading in six years.
  • the network is shrinking, by design.
    management plans to shed another million amazon packages per day in 2026, completing an 18-month exit that will have halved volumes from its largest customer.
  • to align costs, ups expects to cut up to 30,000 operational positions through attrition and a voluntary driver separation program, close at least 24 additional buildings in the first half, and push automation to 68% of u.s. volume by year-end.
    the company also retired its md-11 freighter fleet after a fatal crash, taking a $137 million write-off and leasing 767 capacity as replacements arrive.
source: UPS Q4/FY 2025 earnings release (Jan 27, 2026) · about.ups.com / investors.ups.com
