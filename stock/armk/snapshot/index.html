the number that mattered
The key number was $4.8B of revenue. It grew 6% while EPS slipped 8%, so the business is still expanding, not shrinking.
  • retention rates have also been very strong lately as aramark has kept over 96% of its customers, indicating that quality levels remain high. The momentum is good in all parts of the business.
    in the collegiate hospitality business, the company is benefiting from higher student enrollments, particularly in the south and southeast. and it is seeing success at crossing over its academic food service into collegiate sports, which is experiencing double-digit revenue growth. in corrections, aramark is capitalizing on the growing trend for these institutions to outsource their food preparation service. the overseas business is also doing well, as the company has strengthened its presence with several european sports leagues. considering all of these factors, we look for the company to post profits of $2.25 a share this fiscal year and $2.60 in fiscal 2027. (we note that beginning in fiscal 2026, we are using adjusted earnings and we are not restating prior years. on a comparable basis, the company earned $1.82 in fiscal 2025.) finances are in reasonably good shape.
  • long-term debt got as high as 77% of total capitalization in 2020.
  • but over the past several years, management has whittled away at its total obligations and we expect the debt ratio to decline to less than 60% by the end of this fiscal year.
    the company has no significant debt maturities for the next two years, and it has over $600 million in cash on hand. These neutrally ranked shares have good long-term appeal. over the next 3 to 5 years, we look for the stock to break out of its years-long trading range as the company establishes a more consistent pattern of earnings growth.
