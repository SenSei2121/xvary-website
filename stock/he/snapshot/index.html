the number that mattered
The 27.27% EPS miss mattered because this stock already needs investors to believe the rebound from $0.72 trailing EPS to $1.05 is real.
  • hawaiian electric industries (hei) is making headway on its recovery from the maui wildfire.
    to recap, in august 2023, hurricane level winds toppled power lines, igniting the deadly blaze that destroyed the town of lahaina. hei was overwhelmed with hundreds of tort claims for local damages and insurers’ lawsuits seeking restitution for payments made to policy holders. in a deal brokered by state authorities, the utility is slated to pay nearly half of a $4 billion settlement for local tort claims. the hawaii supreme court ruled that once the settlement is legally finalized, insurers cannot sue hei and other defendants. with that ruling in the company’s favor, the administrative steps needed to finalize the settlement are expected to be completed by the first half of this year, after which hei is due to make its first of four $479 million annual payments.
  • in 2024, hei took a $1.875 billion writeoff related to wildfire tort claims.
  • we included it in our earnings presentation to correlate with the massive drop in the share price.
  • the climb back to respectability remains difficult.
    hei initially contributed $75 million to the state’s wildfires victim fund and owes an additional $1.916 billion.
  • it’s expected to be paid off from this year through mid-2029.
