the number that mattered
EPS of $0.67 mattered most because it beat the $0.52 estimate by 28.85%. That is the market paying up for stronger gold pricing and cleaner execution.
  • as expected, kinross gold delivered a strong third-quarter performance.
  • the miner produced 504,000 gold equivalent ounces (geo) during the period, down sharply from the 564,106 geo recorded in the year-earlier interim.
  • still, robust gold markets helped kinross register impressive top- and bottom-line growth.
    an average realized price of $3,458 per ounce and cost of $1,145 per ounce resulted in a margin of over $2,300 per ounce. the company produced 1.5 million ounces at a cost of nearly $1,090 per ounce during the first nine months of 2025. various factors suggest earnings comparisons will remain mostly favorable during the quarters ahead. continued increases in gold prices, combined with the reliance on low-cost mines and shareholder-friendly policies, augur well for results. while fourth-quarter production is liable to moderate modestly, gold’s vigor has led us to sharply revise upwards our bottom-line outlook for the period.
  • likewise, we have raised our earnings-per-share targets for this year and next to $1.70 and $2.20, respectively.
  • projects have the potential to maintain profit momentum.
    the near-term developmental pipeline, including investments and/or expansion at the round mountain, bald mountain, and great bear mines, should help maintain annual production at 2.0 million ounces through 2030.
