What they do
Energous makes wireless power tech for small IoT devices, so sensors can run with fewer or no batteries.
Energous wins only if battery swaps are expensive enough to justify new hardware. Its pitch is simple: power shelf labels, trackers, and sensors over the air, with just 26 employees focused on that niche. If your devices can avoid battery maintenance, that saves labor; if customers do not care, this edge disappears.

What could go wrong

The #1 risk here is commercial adoption of Energous' wireless power platform. The technology story is finally producing revenue, but it still has not produced a self-funding business.

∘
med
Commercial adoption stalls
Last reported annual revenue was $800K. The page also cites $5.6M of preliminary 2025 revenue and a $1M quarter. Better, yes. Proven, no. If customers do not reorder at scale, the growth story resets fast.
This is a tiny revenue base supporting a ~$26M market cap. A slowdown would hit the valuation case directly.
∘
med
Dilution stays part of the model
Energous raised $23.9M in equity in 2025 while still reporting losses. That bought time, not profitability. If losses continue, another raise is the obvious funding tool.
Every additional equity raise means your slice of the company gets smaller unless the underlying business finally scales with it.
∘
med
Volatility overwhelms the thesis
A 1.6 beta, 5 / 100 price stability score, and a $4–$18 52-week range tell you this stock trades like a venture bet in public markets.
Even if the technology is real, the path for shareholders can be ugly. You can be directionally right and still get wrecked by volatility and timing.
The combined risk picture is simple: you own a ~$26M company with $5.6M of preliminary 2025 revenue, $23.9M of fresh equity capital, and no margin for commercialization to slip.
