What they do
Unifi turns polyester and nylon into yarns, including recycled fibers, that other companies turn into clothes, car interiors, and home products.
Unifi's edge is REPREVE and scale in recycled yarns. That is branding power in a commodity business, which is rare. You are still betting on a weak moat, though: return on capital was just 3.6%, according to the provided company data, so the business is not turning that reputation into much cash.

What could go wrong

the core risk is simple: Unifi sells into a hard business with ~3% gross margin in the latest reported quarter and carries $113M of long-term debt while still losing money. If margin repair stalls, the equity story gets thin fast.
