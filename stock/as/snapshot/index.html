What they do
Amer Sports sells premium outdoor gear, apparel, shoes, and racquet sports equipment through brands like Arc’teryx, Salomon, and Wilson.
Amer wins because it sells identity with gear attached. You do not buy Arc’teryx just for a jacket when the group runs 500-plus stores and reaches you through multiple premium brands. Technical Apparel is 42% of 2024 revenue, while Outdoor Performance adds 35%, so the company is not leaning on one hot product cycle.

the number that mattered
Gross margin at 57.6% matters most because premium brand stories fall apart fast if pricing power disappears.
  • amer sports is executing well on its growth strategy.
    the sporting goods and apparel retailer delivered strong vs. prior year improvement in both earnings and revenue over the last three reported quarters. we anticipate this trend likely continued in the december period, although these results are not scheduled to be released until next month.
  • recent performance has been driven by favorable demand trends across amer’s cornerstone outdoor performance and technical apparel business segments, where leadership projects full-year revenues will be up about 28%-29% and 26%-27%, respectively, versus 2024.
  • solid top-line growth in ball & racquet operations (expected at 10%-11%) was also supportive of the retailer’s encouraging 2025 campaign.
  • we believe the company is well positioned to keep the ball rolling in 2026.
    amer sports is successfully taking share in premium technical sports and outdoor markets, fueled by surging popularity across its top-tier brands. the portfolio is highlighted by its arc’teryx and solomon footwear franchises, where recent marketing efforts and innovation initiatives are clearly resonating with both millennial and generation z consumers. a keen focus on operational efficiency, coupled with further expansion of its direct-to-consumer and e-commerce capabilities, are expected to drive margins higher in 2026 and 2027. also, with the lion’s share of its revenue coming from overseas, the company’s business appears to be somewhat better insulated from adverse tariff-related scenarios. in 2025, roughly a quarter of the retailer’s top line was generated in u.s. markets, driven heavily by the wilson tennis brand.
  • due to its short trading history, the stock remains unranked for timeliness.
