What could go wrong

the #1 risk is NYSE minimum price compliance. This is not abstract dilution-or-macro handwaving. NRDY received a notice on March 5, 2026 after the stock traded below $1.00.

∘
med
NYSE compliance is on a six-month timer
The company has until September 5, 2026 to cure the price deficiency. A stock under $1 does not just look weak — it limits the shareholder base and can force ugly capital-markets decisions.
A failed cure would hit liquidity, credibility, and likely the multiple at the same time.
∘
med
Consumer is growing, institutional is not
Consumer revenue grew 26% while institutional revenue was flat. If the slower segment stays flat, the $180M–$190M revenue guide becomes a lot harder to trust.
Roughly 30% of revenue is tied to the segment that is currently not helping.
∘
med
AI lowers the product cost — and the moat
Nerdy is trying to use AI to improve tutor productivity and margins. The problem is the same technology also makes cheaper alternatives easier to build. In a low-switching-cost category, product improvement and competitive pressure can arrive together.
If pricing weakens, a 66.8% adjusted gross margin can compress before operating leverage fully arrives.
∘
med
Recent insider sales do not help sentiment
The COO sold 18,457 shares in January 2026, and the chief legal officer sold 32,641 shares in February 2026. These are not thesis-breaking numbers, but they are not the signal investors want during a compliance fight.
At this market cap, small trust hits can matter more than small share counts.
A $1.05 stock with a March 5, 2026 delisting notice and a $180M–$190M revenue promise does not get the luxury of slow execution.
