What they do
Shoals sells the wiring and electrical gear that move power from solar panels to batteries, chargers, and the grid.
Shoals wins by moving labor from the field to the factory. That sounds boring until you remember labor is where solar projects lose time and money. Its Big Lead Assembly and plug-and-play EBOS products helped push 2025 revenue to $475.3 million, up 19.1%, because developers want fewer parts, fewer mistakes, and faster installs.

missed estimates
Shoals posted latest-quarter revenue of $327 million, but the last reported EPS of $0.05 missed estimates by 50%.
The weird part is the contrast. Revenue jumped 141% vs. prior year in the latest quarter, while management also said it is accepting lower margins to win more business.

the number that mattered
The 50% EPS miss mattered most because it tells you growth is real, but profit conversion is still messy.
  • shoals technologies reported solid financial results in 2025.
  • the company saw its top line increase 19% vs. prior year, to $475.3 million, driven by strong underlying product demand, the impact of market share capture initiatives, and an increase in project volumes.
  • this resulted in earnings of $0.20 per share, up 43% compared to earnings of $0.14 per share in the prior year.
    overall, the massive increase in u.s. energy consumption is driving an enormous long-term investment cycle in new power generation.
  • management is intentionally accepting lower gross margins to capture a larger total addressable market.
    in order to shift from a narrow product set to a diversified portfolio, shoals is consolidating operations into a new state-of-the-art facility. this consolidation also includes a pivot toward battery energy storage systems (bess) and ai data-center infrastructure.
  • these new markets often involve higher-volume, lower-margin product sets, which will weigh on the gross margins in the near term.
    management recently outlined its path-to-market and competitive positioning for battery solutions, underlining its ambition to capture a share of the rapidly growing energystorage market.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is utility-scale solar project delays and a lower-margin mix shift.

project timing risk

lower-margin expansion risk

impact: with a 13.0% operating margin and 11.0% net margin, there is room for execution error — just not a lot of it.

tariff and supply chain risk

impact: cost inflation lands directly on a company carrying $137M of long-term debt and no wide margin cushion.
