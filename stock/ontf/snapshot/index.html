What could go wrong

The top risk here is the Cvent merger failing to close.

∘
med
Shareholders or regulators block the sale
The $400M all-cash acquisition still needs shareholder approval and closing conditions. The vote is scheduled for March 26, 2026, and the expected close is by Q3 2026.
If the deal breaks, the stock loses the $8.10 anchor and likely falls back toward where it traded before the announcement.
∘
med
The core business keeps shrinking into the close
Revenue fell 5.9% to $139M and ARR ended at $124M. If churn or seat reductions continue, the operating story gets worse even if the balance sheet still looks fine.
That matters because a deteriorating business can complicate integration, drag out timelines, and increase the odds of renegotiation pressure.
∘
med
Pre-IPO churn allegations keep the narrative messy
A class action alleges the company made misleading statements about customer churn and revenue trends before its IPO. That is not the current operating thesis, but it is part of the backdrop.
Extra legal noise rarely helps a company in the middle of a sale process. It adds friction to a stock that already trades on confidence.
At $5.83 versus an $8.10 cash offer, the market is assigning a meaningful probability that closing risk outweighs the spread.
