the number that mattered
72.6% gross margin was the real story, because storage hardware companies are not supposed to print software-like economics this consistently.
  • netapp turned in a solid performance for its october quarter.
  • revenues were up 3% from the prior-year period, to $1.705 billion, above the mid-point of the company’s earlier guidance.
    the datainfrastructure provider experienced some shutdown-related headwinds among public sector customers, but this was offset by broader demand for artificial intelligence (ai) solutions, cloud-storage services, and all-flash offerings.
  • gross margins, meanwhile, surpassed management’s expectations (70.5%-71.5%), expanding 60 basis points, to 72.6% on an adjusted basis.
  • this improvement helped to lift earnings 10% vs. prior year, to $2.05, nicely above netapp’s forecast of $1.84-$1.94.
    the company should be able to continue its progress over the rest of fiscal 2025. (year ends april 24, 2026.) top-line growth will likely remain in the low- to mid-single-digit range. rising activity around ai infrastructure and data storage projects augurs well for future growth, with netapp closing 200 such deals in the recent quarter, up from 125 in the july period. meanwhile, the company has hiked its full-year target for share earnings from $7.60-$7.90 to $7.75-$8.05, largely reflecting the better-than-expected margin performance in the october quarter.
  • we look for profits to climb 7%-9% in fiscal 2026, to roughly $8.50 a share.
    revenues are apt to rise at a mid-single-digit rate, but we are more cautious about the prospect of further expansion in the operating margin. in particular, we will be keeping an eye on memory prices, as this key component of netapp’s cost structure has been on the rise. this shouldn’t impact margins in the current fiscal year, but could become a headwind in subsequent quarters.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is margin compression from rising memory costs.

∘
med
memory cost inflation
Management explicitly called out memory prices as a rising input cost. That matters because the recent beat was driven by gross margin landing at 72.6%.
If that cost line moves the wrong way, the margin beat that lifted EPS above the $1.84–$1.94 range can fade fast.
∘
med
AI momentum may stay niche
AI-related storage deals rose to 200 from 125 in the prior quarter. Good number. Still, the wider revenue outlook is only low- to mid-single-digit growth.
If AI demand does not scale beyond a helpful offset, you are left owning a steady business rather than a re-rating story.
∘
med
enterprise and public-sector spending pauses
The october quarter already saw shutdown-related pressure among public-sector customers. That tells you demand is not immune to budget timing and procurement delays.
When quarterly revenue is $1.705B and growth is 3% from a year ago, even modest delays can become the whole story.
∘
med
upside expectations are not fully aligned
The stock trades at $117.53, while the listed midpoint target is $107. The same page also shows a 3–5 year low target of $140 and high target of $210.
That is not precision. It is disagreement. When the target set looks messy, execution matters more than anyone's neat price box.
A margin-led quarter can support the story for a while, but if gross margin slips and revenue stays in the low- to mid-single digits, the path to the $7.75–$8.05 earnings range gets narrower.
