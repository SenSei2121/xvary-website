the number that mattered
The number that mattered was -$1.03 EPS. Revenue jumped, but the company still lost money on the quarter.
  • enviri likely saw mixed results in the fourth quarter of 2025.
  • we expect revenues were up slightly vs. prior year, by about 1%, but net loss per share likely widened from the year-ago period due to ongoing headwinds in the harsco rail and harsco environmental segments.
  • for the full year, we expect that the picture was more completely negative, with the top line down about 4% and the share loss widening.
    in november, the company announced the sale of its clean earth business to veolia enrionnement sa for $3.04 billion. the proceeds of the sale will go to pay down enviri’s debt and transaction costs, with the remainder, estimated at $14.50 to $16.50 per share, going directly to shareholders. upon the deal’s closing, the company’s remaining segments, harsco rail and harsco environmental, will be spun off into a new company, dissolving enviri as it currently exists.
  • enviri shareholders will receive a third of a share in the new company for each enviri share held.
    it should be noted, however, that clean earth accounts for the majority of the company’s current value, and that any recovery of the harsco businesses post-spin-off is speculative. accordingly, from an investment perspective, the current situation is in many ways more akin to a buyout than a more typical sort of divestiture. per our convention, the impact of this deal is not currently reflected in our estimates, as the transaction has not yet closed. should the deal fall through prior to its estimated mid-2026 closing date, we expect that the share price would fall rapidly, and that it likely would not recover to its current level at any point through the end of the decade barring the announcement of another deal.
  • however, we think that scenario is unlikely.

What could go wrong

this is a closing-risk stock wearing an industrial ticker. The sale, payout, debt cleanup, and spin all have to land in the right order. If one piece slips, the whole setup gets less attractive fast.

∘
med
Clean Earth sale fails to close
The $3.04B Veolia transaction still depends on shareholder approval, antitrust clearance, and an effective registration statement. If the sale breaks, the expected $14.50–$16.50 per-share cash return goes with it.
impact: the main support under today's valuation disappears.
∘
med
The payout lands at the low end or below it
Debt paydown and transaction costs come out before cash gets to you. If those items run heavier than expected, the number investors care about most can shrink even if the sale closes.
impact: a smaller payout leaves you owning more stub risk than the current stock price implies.
∘
med
The remaining business stays weaker than management hopes
Management is targeting $140M in pro forma EBITDA for 2026, but Harsco Rail is expected to burn cash until at least 2027 and the continuing operations lost $160M in 2025. You are being asked to trust a turnaround in the part of the company investors liked less.
impact: even with the sale closed, the stub can trade down if losses and cash burn stick around.
∘
med
The shareholder base looks event-driven, not patient
Institutions were net sellers for three straight quarters, with 87 buyers versus 94 sellers in 3q2025. That is not a vote of confidence in the long-term operating story.
impact: if deal certainty wobbles or the stub disappoints, you may not have sticky holders stepping in.
At $19.26, the market is saying the cash is probably real and the stub is worth something. Here's the thing: the cash is not paid yet, and the stub still comes with a $160M continuing-operations loss in the latest full year.
