What they do
Werner moves freight with 7,450 trucks and about 28,665 trailers across the U.S., Canada, and Mexico.
Werner runs 7,450 trucks and 28,665 trailers. Scale (more rigs and routes) → more lanes covered → so your freight moves without building your own fleet. The FirstFleet deal made Werner the fifth-largest dedicated carrier, and dedicated (longer contracts, steadier work) beats spot freight (load-by-load pricing) when rates wobble.

The $0.09 EPS miss mattered more than the $2.2B revenue print, because $0.05 against $0.14 says profits still lag expectations.
  • werner enterprises’ stock price has benefited from the broader trucking industry rally.
  • the shares are up 42% since our mid-november review.
    investors have likely bid up the shares on the strength of a solid showing to close out 2025 and the likelihood of strained capacity this year.
  • the acquisition of firstfleet should prove fruitful.
    werner acquired tennessee-based firstfleet for $245 million in cash, adding 2,400 tractors and $615 million in annual revenue.
  • the deal makes werner the fifth-largest dedicated carrier nationally, expanding the highermargin dedicated fleet by roughly 50%.
    management expects immediate earnings accretion with $18 million in annual realized within two years through greater network density and purchasing power. the trucker’s december-quarter results were probably more encouraging than those of most of its peer group. our call of $0.14 a share reflects what we believe was a favorable revenue mix and benefits from operational efficiency. dedicated fleet startups that pressured third-quarter margins probably reached productivity, eliminating about $2 million in quarterly startup costs by october. one-way production recovered as driver rebalancing wound down and the fleet became more focused on profitable lanes. peak season volumes from discount retail customers also held up well, while technology-driven cost savings offered further margin relief.
  • the recovery prospects here are compelling.
    projected capacity constraint related to greater federal regulatory enforcement is set to enable meaningful contract rate increases during bid seasons. what’s more, the firstfleet integration is expected to deliver between $15 million to $20 million in, while expanding presence in resilient grocery and bakery markets.
