What they do
Hudbay digs copper, gold, silver, zinc, and molybdenum from mines and plants in Canada, Peru, Arizona, and Nevada.
You are not buying a one-asset miner. Hudbay runs 3 polymetallic mines (one rock, multiple metals) and 4 ore concentrators across Canada and Peru. Copper was 57% of 2024 sales, so the business has scale, but your ride still follows one metal.

  • hudbay minerals missed the mark in the third quarter.
    sales of $346.8 million and earnings of $0.03 per share came in well below our respective targets of $560 million and $0.60 per share due to unexpected factors. depressed third-quarter copper production of 24,000 tonnes and gold output of 54,000 ounces reflected the negative impact of wildfires across parts of northern manitoba for a majority of the third quarter and a temporary production interruption in peru. But wall street chose to look at the big picture. the backdrop has strengthened due to the combination of increasingly robust demand and industrywide supply disruptions, which formed a positive combination.
  • in fact, a roughly 35% surge in copper prices year to date has catapulted the shiny metal to a record level of nearly $12,000 per metric ton.
    a diversified operating base enabled the company to maintain the low end of its consolidated output guidance. this centers around the outlook for two major products: copper (117,000 tonnes-149,000 tonnes) and gold (247,500-308,000 ounces). another positive factor was hudbay's apparent progress in efforts to develop the world-leading copper world project. although we have trimmed our 2025 earnings estimate to account for the recent disappointment, our enhanced target for 2026 reflects strengthening copper markets. Improving financial health augurs well for the company.
  • hudbay closed the third quarter with liquidity of $1.04 billion and moderating levels of debt.
    the former is apt to benefit from the recent approval of the joint venture with mitsubishi to develop the copper world mine.
  • feasibility activities are underway, and a sanction decision is probable in 2026.
  • according to management, copper world's completion has the potential to grow annual copper volumes by more than 50% from current levels. Hudbay shares are up more than 130% year to date.

What could go wrong

HBM is not a vague "miner risk" story. The setup is specific: 57% of revenue comes from copper, the stock is up 130% this year, and the expansion case leans hard on Copper World getting sanctioned in 2026. That makes the upside obvious and the failure points pretty easy to name.
