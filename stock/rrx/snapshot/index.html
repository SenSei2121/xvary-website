the number that mattered
The number that mattered was 53.2% gross margin, because margin strength gives Regal Rexnord more room to absorb the $175 million tariff hit.
  • we look for a stronger year at regal rexnord in 2026.
    results have been rather stagnant at the company over the past several years, but we see growth ahead.
  • a shortage of rare earth magnets appears to be easing.
    these are powerful magnets that are used in the company's industrial motors, and better availability of them should boost sales this year. additionally, regal rexnord manufactures electrical pods that are customizable and modular making them a key component in data center construction. the company has a pipeline of $1 billion of projects to bid on in this realm, and it is adding capacity to support the rising demand. another area of potential growth is humanoids, which need the actuators, micro motors, and precision bearings that regal makes.
  • the company continues to take steps to mitigate tariff impacts.
    since they were first announced last spring, the timing and scale of increased trade sanctions has been uncertain. early on, management expected that the negative impact would be $60 million annually if no action was taken, but they could make moves to neutralize the effects within 2025.
  • as of late october, the unmitigated impact was estimated at $175 million.
    by realigning its supply chain, relocating production, and implementing selected price increases, management expects the tariff impact to be neutral in the first half of 2026.
  • an unexpected change in leadership is forthcoming.
    in october, the board of directors announced that it was commencing a ceo succession plan, without any detail.
