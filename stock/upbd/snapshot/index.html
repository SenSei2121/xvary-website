the number that mattered
Credit quality and Acima charge-off trends mattered as much as the EPS beat—management called out underwriting tightening in 2025 commentary.
  • upbound group’s shares remain out of favor with investors, falling nearly 40% in 2025 to five-year lows.
    the rentto-own stalwart’s core unbanked and underbanked typically earning between $25,000 and $60,000 annually are being squeezed by the cumulative toll of inflation on essentials such as food and energy. because this demographic often lives paycheck to paycheck and relies on alternative financial services due to limited credit access, higher prices have exhausted their disposable income and reduced confidence, making discretionary big-ticket purchases a tough sell. a ‘‘choppy macro backdrop’’ has shifted upbound’s strategic focus from aggressive expansion to rigorous credit quality.
  • in the acima segment, the environment triggered a spike in lease charge-offs in the third quarter, and they were projected to peak at 10% in late 2025.
  • this will be particularly true among new e-commerce customers.
    in response, management implemented drastic underwriting tightening over the summer months to filter out high-risk profiles.
  • the stock’s volatility was on display after the shares plummeted following a lowered full-year outlook.
    this was attributed to margin compression at acima and persistent revenue weakness at rent-a-center.
  • to right the ship, upbound is accelerating a digital transformation, highlighted by a 40% revenue surge in its brigit financial wellness app.
    central to this effort is an ai-powered ‘‘leasability’’ engine that lets customers shop at any retailer by using the app to determine approval limits and pay via a virtual tap-tolease card. furthermore, the company is using projected $150 million in tax savings to reduce leverage and protect its dividend. upbound shares currently offer a compelling entry point for deep value investors as the price remains depressed near historical lows.
