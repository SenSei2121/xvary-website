the number that mattered
The important number was the $0.02 beat, because it says the business can still squeeze out a small win even while annual revenue fell 5.8%.
  • sensata technologies’ acquisition of dynapower is looking like a bust.
  • to wit, the supplier of automotive and industrial sensors recently wrote down the value of its dynapower business by $226 million, equal to nearly 40% of the $580 million that the company originally paid for the developer of microgrid power inversion and rectification technology back in july 2022.
    as we understand it, sensata was obligated to take the non-cash charge, as the applications for dynapower’s knowhow, including electric-vehicle charging stations and hydrogen-fueled power generation, aren’t likely to meet the company’s once-lofty expectations. on the plus side, management identified other potential target markets for dynapower, such as defense and data centers, where grid stabilization and redundant power supply are key.
  • still, the lack of specificity surrounding these new opportunities gives us some pause.
    leadership recently expected sensata’s adjusted 2025 earnings (excluding the dynapower charge and other items) to be little changed from 2024’s $3.44-a-share tally.
  • during a year when overall sales probably fell 6% or so, the company’s ability to largely maintain the level of profit is notable.
    it coincides, in part, with activist investor elliott management urging the company to be a better steward of capital.
  • the top line ought to head in the right direction this year.
    importantly, comparisons should be more normal, as sensata appears nearly done winding down and divesting non-core businesses and product lines.
