What could go wrong

The problem here is specific: FTC Solar has a $69M market cap, only $47M of revenue, 8.6% gross margin, and a fresh technical default issue after Q4. In that setup, one weak link does not stay isolated for long.

∘
med
technical default stops being a footnote
The company entered a technical default after Q4 results. That matters because lenders, suppliers, and customers do not view a small-cap stumble the same way they view a large-cap stumble.
With a C++ balance sheet and a $69M market cap, restricted financing flexibility can take over the whole narrative very quickly.
∘
med
8.6% gross margin leaves almost no room for error
Gross margin of 8.6% means the business keeps less than nine cents of every revenue dollar before overhead. In a project business, one delay, one pricing concession, or one cost miss does real damage.
If margin stays around this level, even better revenue will not create much financial relief.
∘
med
larger rivals have more room to price and wait
FTC Solar is trying to win utility-scale projects in a market where scale matters on procurement, pricing, and execution. $47M of revenue is simply not much firepower.
The risk is straightforward: bigger competitors can bid harder, absorb delays better, and make it difficult for a smaller player to earn its way out of trouble.
A forced financing fix, continued single-digit gross margin, or slower booking conversion all hit the same weak point: this company does not have much cushion between a small revenue base and a stressed capital structure.
