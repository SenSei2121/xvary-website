What they do
TopBuild installs insulation and roofing, then sells those materials through 440 U.S. branches.
TopBuild wins because it does two jobs at once. It installs product through TruTeam, which was about ~59% of revenue on the $3.2B / $5.4B split in the product cards, and it distributes product through a 440-branch network. Vertical integration → it sells and installs the same stuff → so what: you get scale on both labor and materials while smaller rivals usually have one or the other.

the number that mattered
The key number was 2026 EPS guidance of $19.50, down from $19.77 in 2025, because it says the rebound story got pushed out another year.
  • after a decade of rapid growth, earnings declined in the second half of 2025.
    softness in the residential and light commercial markets persists, driven by weak consumer confidence and housing affordability issues.
  • however, we believe there is room for optimism as the year progresses.
  • home mortgage rates recently fell below 6% for the first time in three years, and this should improve buyer psychology as well as boost affordability.
    as demand picks up, management's efforts to optimize the company's cost structure should help margins.
  • we estimate a slight decline in earnings this year, to $19.50 per share, before a solid rebound in 2027, to $22.50 a share. Acquisitions remain a key strategy at topbuild.
    in 2025, the company completed seven acquisitions that will add about $800 million in sales this year. it spent $1.9 billion in cash on these deals, which included suppliers of roofing products, door products, and insulation fabrics. management has been active already in 2026 as well, completing two purchases in february and announcing a third. the goal of these deals is to expand product line breadth, broaden geographical reach, and bolster margins. we expect the company to remain active in pursuing accretive additions for the foreseeable future. The company's diversification is a key strength. over the years, management has broadened the business base to the point that installation and specialty distribution are comparable in size, as is the exposure to residential and commercial markets. this balance provides stability during times of weakness in any one area. These shares are ranked to outpace the market averages in the coming six to 12 months. they have maintained their above average outlook rank (2) since our last report three months ago. we note, however, the stock can be volatile as demonstrated by its 1.25 beta and below par price stability score of 40. over the longer term, we think this issue will be about an average holding as the valuation has increased of late, and the lack of a dividend hurts total return potential.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is U.S. housing starts staying weak while TopBuild still gets about ~59% of revenue from installation on the $3.2B / $5.4B split—distribution is the rest.

!

residential construction downturn
About ~59% of revenue here is the installation line that tracks residential construction most directly. When housing starts weaken, insulation volumes usually follow with them.
A 10% drop in housing starts could cut annual revenue by about $300M.

SPI integration pressure
The $1B SPI acquisition added about $1.2B in annual revenue, but it also weighed on Specialty Distribution margins. More scale only helps if margin recovery shows up next.
Management already flagged a $55M price-cost headwind tied to integration and market conditions.

med
pricing pressure in distribution
Specialty Distribution was flat on $2.2B of revenue shown here, and management cited mix and competitive pricing pressure. That is the wrong place to see softness when the market is already slow.
If pricing stays tight, gross margin has less room to defend the current ~28% level.

expectations are still high

About ~59% of revenue ties to the installation-led mix on this page, the SPI deal carries a $55M price-cost drag, and the stock sits 11% above the average target. Put together, that means execution has to stay strong just to hold the current narrative.
