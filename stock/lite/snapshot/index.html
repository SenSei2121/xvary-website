What they do
Lumentum makes the lasers and optical parts that move data through cloud networks, telecom gear, factories, and sensing systems.
Its parts sit deep inside customer hardware, so swapping vendors is painful once a design is locked. Cloud & Networking made 86% of fiscal 2025 revenue, and R&D ran at 18.5% of sales, which is the company paying to stay inside your customer's box.

the number that mattered
Demand inflection vs a still-negative operating margin — FY revenue growth was ~21%, not a made-up 174% quarter on a $1.6B base.
  • the company posted fiscal first-quarter adjusted earnings of $1.10 a share (versus gaap earnings of a nickel a share), on a hefty 58% jump in revenues.
    the top line reaped the rewards of impressive demand reported by data center, data-center interconnect, and long-haul markets.
  • and leadership expects more than 20% sequential growth in the december interim, and comparisons may improve even more during the latter half of the year, thanks to two of lumentum's next major growth drivers, optical circuit switches and co-packaged optics.
    all told, thanks to its broad optical portfolio, the company seems well positioned to support the impressive expansion of ai (artificial intelligence). finally, we look for a continued improvement in margins, with the fiscal first-quarter adjusted operating margin up 1,570 basis points vs. prior year.
  • all told, we look for the company to post fiscal 2026 adjusted earnings of $5.80 a share, with a strong 20% bottom-line advance likely in the cards for the following year.
  • investors are quite enthusiastic about the company's long-term prospects.
  • to wit, lite stock has jumped more than 70% in value since our early november review, as compared to a relatively flat performance by the s&p 500 index over the same time frame.
