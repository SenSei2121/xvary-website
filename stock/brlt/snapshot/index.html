What they do
Brilliant Earth sells engagement rings and fine jewelry online and through 40 showrooms, with an ethical-sourcing pitch built into the sale.
The edge is trust plus convenience. You can shop online, use virtual try-on, then still walk into one of 40 showrooms before spending thousands. That matters because jewelry is emotional and expensive, and the company has already served customers in over 50 countries.

What could go wrong

Brilliant Earth's risk stack is not abstract. The brand posted record quarterly sales, lost money anyway, and disclosed a material weakness in financial reporting controls.

∘
med
material weakness in financial reporting controls
The company disclosed a material weakness in internal control over financial reporting in 2024. That is a governance problem, not a cosmetic one.
If controls stay weak, you have to discount the precision of every margin and earnings figure on the page.
∘
med
margin compression despite premium pricing
Gross margin fell 370 basis points to 55.9% in the latest quarter, and record sales of $124.4M still produced a $2.9M net loss.
That means even modest cost pressure can erase profitability. For a $128M equity story, that is the whole ballgame.
∘
med
brand differentiation is easier to copy than software
Ethical sourcing helped put Brilliant Earth on the map. Competitors can make similar claims, which leaves pricing and customer acquisition under pressure if the brand message stops standing out.
Full-year sales grew 3.6% to $437.5M in a $350B market. If differentiation weakens, that growth rate can stay stuck while marketing costs rise.
If 55.9% gross margin keeps slipping while sales stay near 3.6%, the stock can still get cheaper from here.
