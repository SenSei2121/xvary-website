What they do
NOV sells the heavy equipment and field services oil and gas companies need to drill, produce, and keep wells running.
This business wins on reach and replacement pain. NOV had 34,010 employees in 2024 and sells into offshore and land markets, so your customer can buy equipment, parts, and support from one vendor. That scale matters when downtime costs real money, even if the return on capital is only 6.0%.

the number that mattered
The key number was the $0.32 EPS miss versus consensus, because it reinforced that 2025 is a transition year, not a clean recovery.
  • nov inc. has been weathering macroeconomic problems well.
  • at the start of last year, crude was $74 a barrel, but it has been beaten down by political and global uncertainties, while saudi arabia, the world's largest oil exporter, announced that it would increase its exports, despite the lower oil prices.
  • economic uncertainties and the impact of tariffs have also been drags.
    the combination of events saw crude fall to $55 a barrel, and nov shares slip to under $11 — both at post-covid lows. the current oil price is marginally above the four-year low; however, nov's cost-control efforts have enabled it to sequentially increase its third-quarter free cash flow to $245 million. this positioned nov to repurchase 6.2 million shares for $80 million and pay regular dividends of $28 million, bringing total capital returned to shareholders in the first three quarters to $393 million, which included a supplemental dividend of $78 million, or $0.21 a share, in the second quarter.
  • earnings should remain a challenge.
  • the fourth quarter of 2025 not only included lower oil and gas prices, but a less-than-usual seasonal increase in the energy equipment segment due to timing of equipment deliveries.
    meanwhile, the impact of tariffs likely rose to $25 million, or about $0.04 a share, for the quarter.
