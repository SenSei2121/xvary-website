What could go wrong

the biggest risk here is not competition in the abstract. it is subscale commercialization with very little financial slack.

∘
med
chronic operating losses
A -74.7% profit margin on $8.93M revenue means the company is still losing money at a rate the business cannot comfortably carry. The gross margin is real. The income statement below it is the problem.
If revenue stays near the current $8.93M run rate, losses keep mattering more than product promise.
∘
med
dental concentration through one channel
The flagship dental business relies on a single strategic partner, Keystone Dental Group, for distribution. When 75% of revenue comes from dental devices, channel concentration is not a side note.
Any disruption there would hit the part of the company that currently pays most of the bills.
∘
med
turnaround timing vs. cash
Management is trying to cut costs under a new CEO while operating with $1.35M in cash and 17 employees. That is enough for urgency, not enough for many mistakes.
A company this small can improve fast, but it can also run out of room before the improvement shows up.
A business doing $8.93M in revenue with $1.35M in cash does not have a wide margin for error. If losses stay deep while the dental channel wobbles, the stock can reprice fast.
