What they do
Globalstar runs satellites that keep phones, trackers, and industrial devices connected where normal cellular networks stop.
Globalstar sells coverage where towers do not exist. That sounds niche until you remember it serves customers in more than 120 countries. You also have 61% insider ownership from officers and directors, which means the people running the company are deeply tied to the same stock you own.

the number that mattered
The 172% revenue jump matters because it shows demand is real, but the negative EPS shows the business model still has to prove it can turn scale into profit.
  • globalstar’s results likely showed improvement last year. (results were released after this report went to press.) recall, third-quarter revenues improved about 10% sequentially, to a record $73.8 million, driven by growth in wholesale capacity services and commercial iot operations.
  • however, expenses ate away at profits, and we think another loss was posted in the final stanza.
  • in the meantime, the company has been focused on several strategic initiatives.
    globalstar is undergoing large capital-intensive projects, such as expanding ground infrastructure for its nextgeneration c-3 satellite constellation. there has also been much anticipation on the company’s plans to bring the hibleoxl-1 satellite system into use. this system represents the foundation for the next era of mobile satellite services by adding new satellites, orbital shells, and frequency bands to extend capacity and enable advanced capabilities for future markets.
  • rumors of a potential takeover have yet to be confirmed.
    recall late last year, there was speculation about a potential sale of globalstar to spacex, a privately-held aerospace, artificial intelligence, and satellite company.
