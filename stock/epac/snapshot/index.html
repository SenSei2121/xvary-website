What they do
Enerpac sells the hydraulic tools and lifting systems you use when something huge, heavy, and expensive has to move safely.
Enerpac sells into jobs where failure is expensive and downtime is worse. You do not swap out a lifting system vendor casually when the load is massive and the schedule is tight. That stickiness shows up in a 50.7% gross margin and a 28.0% operating margin, which is a fancy way of saying a lot of each sales dollar survives after costs.

the number that mattered
The 26% drop in heavy lifting technology mattered most because it erased the benefit of 4% organic product growth.
  • while it&s product sales grew 4% organically and orders improved, these gains were more than offset by a sharp 26% decline in higher-margined service revenue, particularly in the u.k. amid oil and gas customer consolidation.
  • emea and apac revenues fell 10% and 8%, respectively, reflecting macro softness, lumpy heavy lifting technology projects, and political uncertainty in asia.
    tariff-driven cost pressures weighed on margins, and a higher effective tax rate reduced earnings, masking underlying product momentum.
  • that said, we are optimistic about performance picking up pace over the next several quarters.
  • there are multiple tailwinds that should bolster gains for the remainder of the fiscal year.
    order growth remains encouraging across most regions, underpinned by more-stable demand in infrastructure, power generation, and defense. heavy lifting technology continues to build a healthy project pipeline, particularly in apac and the americas. meanwhile, investments in product innovation, commercial capabilities, and e-commerce are expected to improve conversion rates and accelerate product development. what’s more, enerpac’s commercial excellence strategy enhances sales funnel rigor, while a strong backlog and well-positioned specialized products enhance near-term revenue visibility.
  • the next 3 to 5 years are promising, but the stock already reflects much of the gains we envision.
    by our calculations, the company has ample capacity to fund growth initiatives, including m&a, product innovation, and geographic expansion. robust liquidity provides flexibility to invest in more high-return projects and help to buffer against macro volatility. consistent free cash flow generation, disciplined capital allocation, and controlled operating expenses support sustainable earnings growth prospects.

What could go wrong

the #1 risk is another leg down in higher-margin service revenue tied to the u.k. oil and gas channel.

∘
med
service mix keeps deteriorating
Services fell 26% last quarter. Management calls this work higher-margin, so weakness here matters more than the same percentage decline in a lower-margin product line.
If this keeps happening, the 25% operating margin starts looking less durable.
∘
med
regional softness spreads
EMEA revenue fell 10% and APAC fell 8%. Management also cited political uncertainty in Asia and lumpy Heavy Lifting Technology projects.
A project-driven business can absorb one weak region. Two shrinking regions at once is a different conversation.
∘
med
institutional sponsorship is fading
Institutions have been net sellers for three straight quarters. In 3Q2025, 107 funds bought versus 145 that sold.
That does not break the business, but it can cap multiple expansion while investors wait for cleaner growth.
∘
med
cost and tax pressure masks operating progress
Tariff-driven cost pressure and a higher effective tax rate both reduced earnings in the latest period.
Even if product demand improves, margin translation can still disappoint you.
A business growing 4.6% cannot afford its higher-margin service work to keep shrinking 26%. That's the combined risk picture.
