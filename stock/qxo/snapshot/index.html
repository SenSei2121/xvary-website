missed estimates
QXO posted $4.6B in quarterly revenue, but EPS at $0.02 missed the $0.09 estimate by 77.78%.
Revenue rose 70% vs. prior year as acquisitions changed the company's size overnight. The quiet part is margin: gross margin was 22.4%, but the latest-quarter EPS picture remains messy, with Yahoo also showing -$0.46 on a Vs. last year comparison.

the number that mattered
The 77.78% EPS miss matters most because this stock trades on future integration success, and misses make investors question that whole script.
  • qxo, inc. is getting close to completing another significant acquisition.
    nearly one year ago, this enterprise, led by bradley jacobs, acquired beacon roofing for about $11 billion, establishing a core business operation. management is working to bolster this operation’s financial performance via enhanced pricing, productivity & efficiency, supply-chain & distribution processes, product offerings, and geographic market coverage.
  • the fragmented residential & commercial roofing sector presents ample long-term opportunities.
  • mr.
    jacobs looks to capture a larger portion of the $800 billion domestic building supplies sector by increasing the company’s scale.
  • in february, another deal was announced to purchase kodiak building partners for $2.25 billion.
  • kodiak generates $2.4 billion in annual sales, and lifts qxo’s addressable market to $200 billion.
